# Release 0.9 Charter

> Purpose: Define the objective, boundaries, authority, and acceptance conditions for Design Partner Readiness.

## Objective

Prove that Orendalis can safely deliver a trusted, repeatable, founder-supported executive decision journey to ten carefully selected design partners and convert their experience into disciplined product learning.

## Primary Questions

1. Can an executive complete the journey without losing trust or requiring technical guidance?
2. Does Atlas improve decision clarity while preserving uncertainty and executive agency?
3. Does the executive return because the product remains useful after the first assessment?
4. Can the founder support, learn from, and protect ten partners without operational overload?
5. Are the product, support, privacy, recovery, and learning processes repeatable enough for Private Beta?

## In Scope

- qualification and cohort composition;
- founder-approved invitations and onboarding;
- one complete opportunity-decision journey per partner;
- structured and moderated feedback;
- return-use observation;
- safe issue and learning management;
- founder operating cadence;
- quantitative and qualitative success measures;
- graduation or pause decision.

## Out of Scope

- public registration or public launch;
- marketing, testimonials, press, or referrals;
- billing or commercial commitments;
- automation, AI-provider integration, live job discovery, or new modules;
- employment, legal, financial, psychological, or hiring-outcome advice;
- importing unnecessary sensitive documents;
- production expansion or general availability.

## Authority

The founder approves each participant, each cohort wave, any real-data use, external messages, material remediation, and the final Private Beta decision. Product, security, privacy, and release gates remain independent of schedule pressure.

## Release Gates

| Gate | Evidence required |
| --- | --- |
| Product readiness | Required Release 0.9 corrections pass validation |
| Security and privacy | Isolation, invitation, recovery, lifecycle, and data notices accepted |
| Partner readiness | Candidate qualifies and provides informed participation consent |
| Wave readiness | Prior wave reviewed; blockers closed or explicitly stopped |
| Private Beta readiness | Exit criteria and founder decision recorded |

## Definition of Done

The design package is complete when all operating documents are internally consistent, application needs are classified without implementation, safety and evidence boundaries are explicit, and the release decision is recorded. Program execution is a later approved phase.
