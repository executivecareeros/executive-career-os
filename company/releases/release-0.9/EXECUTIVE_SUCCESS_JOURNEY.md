# Executive Success Journey

> Purpose: Define the complete experience that every Release 0.9 design partner should understand and be able to complete.

## Success Promise

The partner should leave with a clearer view of one consequential opportunity, a preserved decision record, explicit unknowns, and a practical next conversation—without feeling manipulated, exposed, or replaced by software.

## Journey

### 1. Orientation

The founder explains program purpose, boundaries, data mode, time commitment, support, observation, withdrawal, and the distinction between product guidance and professional advice.

**Success:** the partner can explain what Orendalis and Atlas will and will not do.

### 2. Secure Access

The partner accepts a single-use invitation, verifies email, creates their account, and enters an isolated workspace.

**Success:** access works without founder database intervention; no other workspace is visible.

### 3. Workspace and Trust

The product explains privacy, memory, evidence, and executive control before requesting career context.

**Success:** the partner knows what is stored, why it matters, and how to stop.

### 4. Professional History

The partner confirms only the minimum history needed for the decision. Manual entry or supported document review may be used under the approved data mode.

**Success:** confirmed facts remain distinguishable from missing or unverified information.

### 5. Executive Blueprint

The partner records direction, priorities, constraints, compensation boundary, geography, travel, and executive principles relevant to the decision.

**Success:** the partner recognizes the Blueprint as their instructions to Atlas, not a personality judgment.

### 6. Opportunity Context

The partner enters one real or sanitized opportunity without unsupported facts.

**Success:** known facts, claims, unknowns, and personal constraints remain separate.

### 7. Atlas Assessment

Atlas presents recommendation, confidence, evidence, questions, conflicts, trade-offs, alternatives, and what could change the view.

**Success:** the partner can explain why Atlas reached its position and challenge it.

### 8. Executive Decision

The partner chooses Apply, Wait, Reject, Monitor, Negotiate, or Network First and understands that the decision is theirs.

**Success:** the decision, evidence snapshot, Career Ledger event, and next action are preserved.

### 9. Feedback

The partner records what increased or reduced trust, what was unclear, what was useful, and where Atlas was wrong or incomplete.

**Success:** feedback is isolated, consented, actionable, and visible to the founder without technical access.

### 10. Return Visit

Within seven days, the partner returns to review the briefing, outstanding questions, task, or changed evidence.

**Success:** the product remembers the decision accurately and provides a reason to return.

### 11. Lifecycle Understanding

The partner is shown how to request export, correction, deletion review, consent withdrawal, or account closure. A controlled sample verifies the process during the cohort; every participant need not close their account.

**Success:** rights and retention boundaries are understandable and operational.

## Founder Intervention Rule

The founder may explain purpose or terminology but must not complete fields, choose the decision, coach the participant toward agreement with Atlas, or conceal a defect. Intervention is recorded as journey friction.
