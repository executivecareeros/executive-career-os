# Product Learning Process

> Purpose: Turn ten design-partner journeys into disciplined product decisions without overgeneralizing from a small cohort.

## Learning Backlog

Before each wave, rank no more than five open questions. Each question states why it matters, evidence needed, current confidence, and the decision it could change.

Core questions:

1. Which moment creates or destroys trust fastest?
2. Does Atlas improve clarity rather than merely summarize information?
3. Which missing evidence most often changes the decision?
4. What creates a credible reason to return?
5. Which founder interventions must become product clarity before Private Beta?

## Session Processing

Within one operating day:

1. secure and classify observations;
2. separate defects, usability, trust, requests, and strategic insights;
3. update affected learning questions;
4. identify safety or release blockers;
5. record a provisional response without expanding scope.

## Synthesis

After each wave:

- compare evidence across participants and contexts;
- distinguish repeated pattern, context-specific finding, outlier, and unknown;
- report counts, not percentages, for the ten-person cohort;
- preserve disagreement and negative evidence;
- identify what changed from the prior wave;
- make continue, pause, stop, clarify, polish, or return-to-development decisions.

## Prioritization

Order work by:

1. safety, privacy, isolation, and data integrity;
2. misleading or unexplainable Atlas behavior;
3. blocked core journey;
4. trust and executive agency;
5. repeated clarity or navigation friction;
6. recurring value and willingness to return;
7. feature requests.

## Decision Discipline

Do not claim product-market fit, career outcomes, statistical significance, or causal impact. Ten partners can show whether the operating model is trustworthy and worth continuing; they cannot validate a market.

## Outputs

Each wave produces:

- wave scorecard;
- issue and trust summary;
- learning-question update;
- product decision record;
- residual risks;
- founder continue/pause/stop decision;
- changes required before the next invitation.
