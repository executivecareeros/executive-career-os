# Feedback Operating Model

> Purpose: Convert design-partner experience into safe, traceable product evidence without turning opinion into fact.

## Evidence Channels

1. **In-product feedback:** structured category, severity, workflow stage, description, expected behavior, and follow-up consent.
2. **Founder observation:** actions, hesitation, navigation, errors, assistance, and direct participant language recorded under a participant code.
3. **Session debrief:** trust, clarity, usefulness, disagreement, willingness to return, and value conversation.
4. **Return evidence:** whether and why the partner returned, what changed, and whether the preserved context remained useful.
5. **Support evidence:** issues raised through the approved support channel.

## Capture Rules

- Record behavior separately from interpretation.
- Use the participant’s words sparingly and only with consent; avoid unnecessary personal detail.
- Link product version or revision, environment, route, journey stage, and timestamp.
- Record founder assistance as a friction event.
- Do not infer satisfaction from completion or silence.
- Never place raw feedback, names, email addresses, screenshots with personal data, or invitation links in Git.

## Classification

Use: Bug, Confusion, Friction, Missing Information, Trust Concern, Incorrect Assessment, Useful Insight, Feature Request, Performance, Accessibility, Privacy, or Security.

Use the established Critical, High, Medium, Low, and Suggestion severity model. Privacy, security, cross-workspace access, history mutation, and misleading Atlas claims leave normal triage and enter the incident or stop process.

## Triage

1. acknowledge and secure the record;
2. classify evidence and severity;
3. reproduce where appropriate with fictional data;
4. identify affected partner, wave, journey stage, and release revision safely;
5. decide fix now, clarify, observe, backlog, reject, or escalate;
6. name owner and review date;
7. communicate only when follow-up consent exists;
8. close with verification and learning impact.

## Learning Unit

Every synthesis item contains:

- observation;
- evidence type and count;
- affected context;
- consequence for trust or decision quality;
- confidence;
- competing interpretation;
- proposed response;
- owner and decision.

Feature request volume is not a vote. Repeated friction may reveal a deeper problem, while one severe trust failure may outweigh ten positive comments.
