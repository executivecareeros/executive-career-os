# Release 0.9 Risk and Data Boundaries

> Purpose: Protect design partners and Orendalis while operating a high-trust, founder-led cohort.

## Risk Register

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Control | Stop trigger |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Executive shares unnecessary sensitive data | Medium | High | Data-mode briefing, minimum fields, no email documents | Unapproved sensitive data enters the workflow |
| Atlas is mistaken for advice or authority | Medium | High | Explainability, uncertainty, executive decision language | Partner relies on Atlas as professional advice |
| Cross-workspace disclosure | Low | Critical | RLS, negative authorization tests, incident procedure | Any unauthorized record visibility |
| Founder support overload | Medium | High | 2–3–5 waves, capacity check, invitation cap | Support or triage cannot meet internal objectives |
| Feedback becomes feature voting | Medium | Medium | Evidence synthesis and decision protocol | Scope expands without learning rationale |
| Invitation link exposure | Low | High | Single-use, expiry, email binding, revocation | Suspected forwarding or public disclosure |
| Legal/privacy material differs from behavior | Medium | High | Pre-wave review and factual data map | Notice or consent is inaccurate |
| Small cohort is overinterpreted | High | Medium | Counts, context, no causal or market claims | External claim exceeds evidence |
| Founder bias shapes participant answers | Medium | Medium | Neutral guide and intervention logging | Repeated coaching toward desired outcome |
| Staging/provider outage interrupts a session | Medium | Medium | Precheck, reschedule, incident path | Integrity or access cannot be verified |

## Data Modes

Each wave has one founder-approved mode:

- **Fictional:** no real career or opportunity information.
- **Sanitized:** real decision structure with organizations, people, amounts, or sensitive constraints removed or generalized.
- **Approved personal:** only after factual privacy, legal, lifecycle, security, and operational gates pass.

Do not mix modes silently. Real-data readiness is not inferred from staging availability.

## Data Minimization

Collect only what is necessary for the decision journey and learning question. Do not place participant identity, raw feedback, career documents, compensation, family constraints, account details, invitation links, or screenshots containing personal information in Git.

## Consent Boundaries

Participation consent, product terms, observation consent, follow-up consent, and marketing/testimonial consent are separate. Participation may be withdrawn without pressure. Withdrawal does not promise deletion of security or legal records that must be retained; explain the boundary.

## Founder Access

Founder access is limited to the approved support, invitation, feedback, lifecycle, and operational purposes. Technical access is not permission to browse a partner’s career records.
