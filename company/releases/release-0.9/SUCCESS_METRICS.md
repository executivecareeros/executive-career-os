# Release 0.9 Success Metrics

> Purpose: Define evidence that the design-partner operating model is useful, trusted, and repeatable without vanity metrics.

## Metric Rules

- Report exact counts with denominator and missing data.
- Record source, release revision, timestamp, and data classification.
- Do not treat missing events as failure without investigation.
- Do not claim causality or statistical significance.
- Invitation acceptance is operational information, not product value.

## Primary Measures

| Measure | Definition | Evidence source | Release threshold |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Safe core-journey completion | Partner completes orientation through preserved decision without a Critical/High trust or security failure | Journey state and session record | At least 8 of 10 invited-and-accepted partners, or all participants if program stops below ten for non-product reasons |
| Atlas comprehension | Partner can explain recommendation, key evidence, unknowns, and personal decision authority | Post-assessment question | At least 7 completed partners |
| Decision clarity improvement | Post-assessment clarity exceeds pre-assessment clarity on the same 1–5 question | Pre/post response | At least 6 completed partners; no claim of causality |
| Trust to continue | Partner states they would use Orendalis again for a consequential decision | Debrief | At least 6 completed partners |
| Return value | Partner returns within seven days and identifies a useful reason for returning | Authenticated return and debrief | At least 5 completed partners |
| Founder intervention | Core journey requires product-explanation or technical rescue | Observation | Declines by the final wave; no repeated blocker requiring founder workaround |
| Safety | Critical security, privacy, isolation, history, or misleading-Atlas defect | Incident and issue records | Zero unresolved Critical; zero unresolved High affecting the core journey |

## Supporting Measures

- qualified, invited, accepted, expired, revoked, withdrawn, completed, and returned counts;
- time from accepted invitation to first successful session;
- onboarding and journey abandonment stage;
- evidence inspected before decision;
- Atlas disagreement and whether disagreement was supported;
- open questions resolved before action;
- feedback submission and follow-up consent;
- support cases by severity and time to safe response;
- lifecycle requests completed correctly;
- founder hours per active partner and per completed journey;
- defects introduced or repeated by wave.

## Qualitative Evidence

For each completed partner, record:

- what changed their understanding;
- what made Atlas credible or untrustworthy;
- what they would have done without Orendalis;
- what they expected but did not receive;
- what would make them return;
- whether they would recommend a confidential trial and why.

## Interpretation

Thresholds are release gates, not proof of market demand. Failure identifies where the operating or product model needs work. Success authorizes only a founder decision about a separately defined Private Beta.
