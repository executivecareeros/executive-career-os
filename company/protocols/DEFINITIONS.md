# ODS Definitions

> Purpose: Provide a shared vocabulary for consistent Orendalis execution and reporting.

- **Acceptance:** Accountable confirmation that defined criteria and required evidence passed.
- **Accountable owner:** The single person answerable for a decision or outcome.
- **Approval gate:** A point beyond which work cannot proceed without named authority.
- **Atlas:** Orendalis decision intelligence that supports, but never replaces, executive judgment.
- **Authoritative record:** The designated source for current truth in a subject area.
- **Blocker:** A condition preventing safe or authorized progress.
- **Confirmed evidence:** Information explicitly verified or accepted through the applicable process.
- **Decision:** A committed choice among alternatives, owned and preserved.
- **Deployment:** Installation of a revision in an environment; not equivalent to acceptance.
- **Environment:** A separated development, staging, or production operating boundary.
- **Exception:** Approved, bounded, temporary deviation from protocol.
- **Founder approval:** Explicit, current, action-specific authorization by the founder.
- **Material change:** A change affecting customers, production, data, security, privacy, cost, law, ownership, architecture, or company commitments.
- **Protocol:** A mandatory operating rule and sequence under ODS.
- **Residual risk:** Known risk remaining after controls and validation.
- **Rollback:** Controlled restoration to a verified prior state.
- **Runbook:** Step-by-step procedure for a defined operational trigger.
- **Scope:** The approved objective, included work, exclusions, and boundaries.
- **Superseded:** Replaced by a newer authoritative record while retained as history.
- **Validation:** Evidence-producing checks that determine whether criteria are met.
- **Verification:** Confirmation that a specified fact or condition is true.
