# Executive Opportunity Universe Directive

## Status

- **Authority:** Permanent Founder Product Directive
- **Effective:** 14 July 2026
- **Applies to:** product, engineering, data, operations, security, legal review, and every opportunity provider
- **Precedence:** Supersedes provider-specific product assumptions and provider-by-provider approval rules; remains subject to law, contracts, security, privacy, and explicit later founder decisions

## Strategic Objective

The Executive Opportunity Universe is a strategic Orendalis asset. Orendalis will continuously discover, evaluate, and support every technically feasible, legally appropriate, high-value opportunity ecosystem that materially increases executive opportunity coverage. No illustrative provider list is a product boundary.

Executives should not need to visit or understand individual provider ecosystems. They should trust Orendalis to build the most comprehensive appropriate universe possible while preserving source evidence for verification and audit.

## Non-Negotiable Rules

1. Every provider plugs into the common Opportunity Coverage Engine through the provider adapter contract.
2. Provider logic may collect source records but may not create domain Opportunities directly.
3. Every record passes through validation, normalization, canonical identity resolution, duplicate detection, source attribution, freshness, lifecycle, monitoring, error recovery, and quality measurement.
4. One real-world role is represented by one canonical Opportunity, even when observed through multiple sources.
5. Atlas reasons about the canonical Opportunity and its combined evidence, never a provider-specific duplicate.
6. Uncertain identity matches remain separate and reviewable; Orendalis must not trade false merges for superficial deduplication rates.
7. Source provenance remains available as evidence, but provider names are not the primary executive experience.

## Provider Evaluation Standard

Every proposed provider is assessed consistently for:

1. executive opportunity coverage;
2. executive relevance;
3. data quality;
4. freshness;
5. legal and contractual compliance;
6. reliability;
7. scalability; and
8. engineering efficiency.

Provider count is never a success metric by itself. Within the standing delegation, an adapter may be autonomously approved after its access method, evaluation, and absence of reserved founder gates are recorded. Disabled or evaluation-stage adapters must not collect production records.

## Architecture Invariants

- Provider identifiers are open and are not limited by the initial source catalog.
- Provider discovery is catalog-driven; the Coverage Engine and domain model do not branch on provider names.
- Stable company identity can be added by enrichment and is used conservatively across provider labels.
- Source observations merge into the existing canonical record while retaining their provenance.
- The canonical record owns executive state, Atlas assessment, decisions, and lifecycle.
- Adding a compliant provider must require only an adapter and registration, not an engine or domain redesign.

## Change Control

Provider discovery and compliant adapter work proceed autonomously inside the existing Coverage Engine. No provider-by-provider approval is required.

Founder approval is reserved for paid licensing, contractual acceptance, explicit provider-term acceptance, legal or compliance uncertainty, material personal-data change, material infrastructure cost, fundamental architecture change, or a changed user-facing commercial commitment. Provider-specific exceptions require a documented ODS decision. External credentials and production activation remain subject to the applicable security and release gates.
