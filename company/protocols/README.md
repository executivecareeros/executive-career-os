# Orendalis Development System 1.0

> Purpose: Define the permanent operating protocols for building, validating, releasing, securing, documenting, and governing Orendalis.

## Status

- **Name:** Orendalis Development System
- **Version:** ODS 1.0
- **Authority:** Founder-approved company protocol framework
- **Applies to:** founders, employees, contractors, engineers, designers, product leaders, reviewers, operators, and AI agents
- **Review cadence:** quarterly and after any material failure, incident, or governance change

## Operating Promise

Orendalis work must be intentional, evidence-led, secure, reversible where possible, and honest about uncertainty. Completion is earned through validation. Authority is never inferred from capability. A working system is not automatically a trustworthy system, and a deployed change is not automatically an accepted change.

## How to Use ODS

1. Read this index and the ODS Constitution.
2. Classify the proposed work and identify its decision owner.
3. Apply the Work Lifecycle and every domain protocol relevant to the change.
4. Stop at an approval gate when authority, cost, production, security, privacy, legal, external communication, or irreversibility is involved.
5. Validate proportionately to risk and preserve evidence.
6. Update authoritative records and close with a truthful status.

## Precedence

When instructions conflict, use this order:

1. law, contractual duties, and immediate human safety;
2. explicit current founder decision;
3. ODS Constitution and ODS protocols;
4. approved architecture decisions, security boundaries, and company policies;
5. current runbooks and release plans;
6. task-specific instructions;
7. defaults, conventions, and personal preference.

The higher authority does not silently erase the lower record. Record the conflict, decision, reason, and resulting update.

## Relationship to Existing Records

ODS governs *how work is controlled*. Existing documents retain their specialist purpose:

- `company/operations/` contains operating procedures, readiness records, and runbooks;
- `docs/adr/` contains durable architecture decisions;
- `docs/` contains product and technical architecture;
- `company/founders/` contains founder principles and strategic decisions;
- `company/history/` preserves what happened;
- `company/product/` contains roadmap and product planning.

ODS does not replace these records. It defines how they are created, approved, used, superseded, and audited.

## Minimum Completion Standard

No work is complete until scope, implementation or artifact, validation, residual risk, documentation, decision status, repository state, and next action are all explicit. “Done,” “fixed,” “secure,” “healthy,” and “ready” require evidence.
