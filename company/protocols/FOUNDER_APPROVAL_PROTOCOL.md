# Founder Approval Protocol

> Purpose: Define when explicit founder approval is required and what must be presented before approval.

## Mandatory Founder Gates

Explicit founder approval is required before:

- purchase, paid checkout, subscription, financial commitment, or material overage exposure;
- production deployment, production migration, destructive data action, or irreversible change;
- domain, DNS, email routing, identity provider, authentication, authorization, or recovery change;
- creating, rotating, revoking, or exposing privileged credentials;
- changing repository ownership, visibility, branch protection, organization security, or external access;
- customer invitation, public communication, marketing, legal acceptance, or company announcement;
- sending an external message or filing a support request in the founder’s name;
- collecting new sensitive data or changing consent, retention, export, or deletion behavior;
- introducing a new database, backend, AI provider, automation, or external integration, except an opportunity provider that satisfies the standing Executive Opportunity Universe delegation below;
- changing company mission, category, brand, ownership, pricing, or binding policy;
- granting an ODS exception with material risk.

## Approval Brief

Before the gate, present:

- purpose and exact action;
- scope and affected systems;
- current state and evidence;
- cost, billing frequency, taxes, renewal, and cancellation when applicable;
- permissions and data access requested;
- security, privacy, legal, customer, and operational risks;
- alternatives, including no action;
- rollback and what cannot be reversed;
- founder action required;
- success and stop conditions.

## Valid Approval

Approval must be explicit, current, and specific to the action. Silence, prior approval for another phase, a browser being open, credentials being available, or a general mission statement is not approval.

Material changes are executed one approved phase at a time when requested. After each phase: validate, document, report, and stop.

## Standing Delegation: Executive Opportunity Providers

The founder permanently authorizes autonomous discovery, evaluation, classification, adapter implementation, normalization, deduplication, enrichment, coverage improvement, quality improvement, and monitoring improvement for opportunity providers inside the existing Executive Opportunity Coverage Engine.

An individual provider does **not** require a founder gate when all of the following are true:

- access is free and creates no material infrastructure cost;
- no contract or provider terms require founder acceptance;
- legal and compliance status is sufficiently clear;
- personal-data processing does not materially change;
- the existing Coverage Engine and security boundaries remain intact; and
- no user-facing commercial commitment changes.

Founder approval remains mandatory when paid licensing, contractual acceptance, explicit provider-term acceptance, legal or compliance uncertainty, a material personal-data change, material infrastructure cost, fundamental architecture change, or a user-facing commercial commitment applies. This delegation does not authorize production credentials, production activation, scraping contrary to applicable terms, or bypassing access controls.

## Revocation and Change

The founder may revoke approval before completion. If scope, cost, risk, permissions, provider terms, or irreversible consequences materially change, the original approval expires and a new gate is required.
