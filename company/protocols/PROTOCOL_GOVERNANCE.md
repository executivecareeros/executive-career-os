# Protocol Governance

> Purpose: Control how ODS is interpreted, reviewed, amended, excepted, and superseded.

## Ownership

The founder is accountable for ODS. Functional owners maintain the protocols in their areas. Every contributor must follow the current approved version.

## Versioning

- Major version: changes authority, constitutional principles, or lifecycle obligations.
- Minor version: adds or materially strengthens a protocol without changing constitutional authority.
- Patch version: clarifies language, links, or templates without changing obligations.

The version and approval status are updated in `README.md`. Historical Git records preserve prior versions.

## Amendment Process

1. identify evidence or failure motivating the change;
2. describe affected protocols and behavior;
3. assess security, privacy, operational, and cultural consequences;
4. consult affected owners;
5. obtain founder approval for material changes;
6. update all conflicting references in one coherent change;
7. validate and record effective date.

## Exceptions

An exception records protocol, scope, owner, reason, evidence, risk, compensating control, approval, start, expiry or review date, and closure. Exceptions do not silently become precedent. Emergency necessity may permit temporary deviation only when delay creates greater harm; retrospective review is mandatory.

## Audit

Quarterly, verify:

- protocol links and ownership;
- alignment with current architecture and operations;
- unresolved exceptions;
- approval and release evidence;
- recurring defects or incidents suggesting protocol failure;
- stale, duplicate, or conflicting authoritative records;
- AI-agent and contractor compliance;
- training or onboarding gaps.

## Interpretation

Apply the Constitution’s purpose, not loopholes in wording. When two protocols conflict, prefer the rule that better protects people, data, explicit authority, evidence, reversibility, and historical truth; escalate material ambiguity to the founder and record the resolution.
