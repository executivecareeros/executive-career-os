# ODS 1.0 Validation Record

> Purpose: Record the internal completeness and validation of the initial Orendalis Development System protocol framework.

## Scope

ODS 1.0 was reviewed as a governance system for engineering, product development, quality assurance, releases, documentation, security, privacy, founder approvals, AI-agent behavior, incidents, evidence, and decision-making.

## Coverage

The framework contains:

- constitutional principles and precedence;
- authority, accountability, delegation, and stop authority;
- a complete work lifecycle;
- decision and founder-approval gates;
- AI-agent behavioral and repository rules;
- product, engineering, QA, security, privacy, release, documentation, and incident protocols;
- evidence and completion-reporting standards;
- governance, amendment, exception, versioning, and audit rules;
- shared definitions;
- an execution checklist and reusable templates.

## Internal Consistency Review

- ODS preserves existing specialist runbooks, architecture records, founder records, product plans, and company history.
- Precedence and conflict resolution are explicit.
- Approval does not arise from access, capability, ambient browser state, or prior unrelated approval.
- Deployment, validation, acceptance, and completion are distinct states.
- Production, staging, secrets, personal data, and irreversible actions have explicit controls.
- AI agents are execution aids and cannot create authority or facts.
- Historical records are superseded rather than silently erased.

## Repository Validation

- Scope is limited to `company/protocols/`.
- Every Markdown document contains a purpose statement.
- Internal Markdown links resolve.
- Whitespace validation passes.
- Secret-pattern review found no credential material.
- No application, database, infrastructure, deployment, or provider state was changed.

## Decision

**ODS 1.0 is internally complete for adoption as the governing development protocol framework of Orendalis.**

The framework must be reviewed quarterly and amended through the Protocol Governance process when company scale, law, architecture, operating evidence, or material incidents require change.
