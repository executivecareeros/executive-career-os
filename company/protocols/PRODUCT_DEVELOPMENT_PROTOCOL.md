# Product Development Protocol

> Purpose: Ensure Orendalis product work improves executive decisions without sacrificing trust, clarity, or coherence.

## Product Test

Every proposed capability must answer:

- Which executive decision or operating need improves?
- What evidence shows the problem matters?
- Why does Orendalis own this problem?
- What is the smallest useful outcome?
- What information is required, and is it justified?
- How will the recommendation or output remain explainable?
- What happens when data is missing or confidence is low?
- How does the executive retain control?

## Lifecycle

1. **Problem:** document user, context, consequence, and evidence.
2. **Outcome:** define observable value without prescribing unnecessary architecture.
3. **Scope:** state minimum experience and explicit non-goals.
4. **Trust review:** assess privacy, evidence, explainability, agency, and misuse.
5. **Experience design:** define hierarchy, language, accessibility, responsive behavior, loading, empty, error, and recovery states.
6. **Acceptance:** specify functional, executive-experience, security, and quality criteria.
7. **Delivery:** follow Engineering and QA protocols.
8. **Learning:** collect factual observations and update the backlog without silently expanding the release.

## Executive Experience Standard

The user should know where they are, what matters, what changed, what remains uncertain, what Atlas used, what to do next, and who makes the decision. Avoid engineering language, false urgency, fabricated certainty, manipulative engagement, and decorative complexity.

## Atlas Standard

Atlas must use confirmed evidence, expose uncertainty, ask decision-improving questions, explain recommendations, show what could change the view, and preserve executive agency. Generic chat behavior is not a substitute for decision intelligence.

## Scope Control

Ideas discovered during delivery enter the appropriate backlog unless required to correct a verified blocker within the approved outcome. Product polish must not become architecture expansion; architecture work must not disguise feature creation.
