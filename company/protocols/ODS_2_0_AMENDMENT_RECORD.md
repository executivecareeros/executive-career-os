# ODS 2.0 Amendment Record

## Decision

On 14 July 2026, the Founder granted standing autonomous authority to expand the Executive Opportunity Universe inside the existing Executive Opportunity Coverage Engine. This authority supersedes the former requirement for provider-by-provider approval.

## Reason

Opportunity coverage is a strategic product asset. Requiring approval for every compliant provider would constrain coverage without adding proportionate protection when the access method is free, lawful, contractually clear, operationally contained, and architecturally compatible.

## Affected Protocols

- The ODS Constitution now recognizes explicit bounded standing delegation.
- The Founder Approval Protocol exempts compliant opportunity providers from the general new-provider gate.
- The Executive Opportunity Universe Directive defines autonomous scope and reserved founder gates.
- The provider adapter contract records reserved gate reasons and prevents a gated adapter from being approved autonomously.

## Reserved Founder Authority

Founder approval remains required for paid licensing, contractual acceptance, explicit provider-term acceptance, legal or compliance uncertainty, material personal-data change, material infrastructure cost, fundamental architecture change, or a user-facing commercial commitment change.

## Security, Privacy, and Operational Effect

The amendment does not authorize credentials, production activation, access-control bypass, prohibited collection, or new personal-data processing. Existing security, privacy, QA, release, evidence, and production gates remain in force.

## Validation

Completion requires protocol consistency checks, adapter gate enforcement tests, existing provider-pack regression tests, ingestion and coverage-engine tests, lint, TypeScript validation, and a production build. Git history preserves the superseded ODS 1.0 rule.
