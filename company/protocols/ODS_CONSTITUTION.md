# ODS Constitution

> Purpose: Establish the binding principles that govern all Orendalis development and operational work.

## Articles

1. **Mission governs execution.** Work must improve trusted executive decision-making or the company’s ability to deliver it responsibly.
2. **The executive owns the career.** Orendalis and Atlas serve; they do not take final career authority.
3. **Authority is explicit.** Access, technical ability, or prior permission does not imply unlimited authority.
4. **Evidence precedes claims.** A result is reported only when directly observed or clearly identified as inferred, estimated, or unknown.
5. **Trust precedes speed.** Security, privacy, accuracy, and informed consent are not traded for delivery velocity.
6. **Scope is a boundary.** Complete the approved objective without adding adjacent features, systems, providers, or commitments.
7. **History is preserved.** Material decisions, career records, audits, incidents, and approvals are not silently rewritten.
8. **Uncertainty is visible.** Assumptions, missing evidence, limitations, and confidence must be stated.
9. **Changes are proportionate.** Use the smallest safe change that satisfies the objective.
10. **Reversibility is designed.** Material changes require a rollback or an explicit statement that reversal is impossible.
11. **Production is protected.** Staging success does not authorize production change.
12. **Data is minimized.** Collect, expose, transfer, and retain only what is necessary and authorized.
13. **Secrets remain secret.** Credentials, tokens, recovery factors, private links, and sensitive personal data never enter source control, reports, screenshots, or conversation logs.
14. **Quality is evidence.** Passing compilation alone is not acceptance; test the behavior, risk, accessibility, and user journey relevant to the change.
15. **Failures stop progression.** A failed gate prevents dependent work until resolved or explicitly accepted by the proper authority.
16. **AI agents remain accountable tools.** They may analyze and execute within scope but may not invent authority, facts, approvals, or success.
17. **Human judgment governs material outcomes.** The founder or named accountable owner approves irreversible, costly, public, legal, security-sensitive, or customer-impacting actions.
18. **Documentation is part of the system.** A material change without an updated authoritative record is incomplete.
19. **Exceptions are temporary and visible.** Every exception has an owner, rationale, risk, expiry or review date, and compensating control.
20. **Learning closes the loop.** Defects, incidents, reversals, and customer evidence improve protocols, tests, and decisions.
21. **Standing delegation is explicit and bounded.** A permanent founder directive may authorize recurring work within a named system without repeated approval; every reserved gate, legal duty, security boundary, and stop condition remains binding.

## Constitutional Test

Before proceeding, ask:

- Is the purpose aligned with the mission?
- Is the authority explicit?
- Is the evidence sufficient?
- Is the scope contained?
- Are people, data, and production protected?
- Is the next action reversible or properly approved?
- Will the permanent record remain truthful?

If any answer is no or unknown, stop at the appropriate gate.
