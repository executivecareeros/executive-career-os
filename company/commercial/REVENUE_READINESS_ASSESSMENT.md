# Revenue Readiness Assessment

> Purpose: Determine whether Orendalis can confidently charge its first executive customer and identify every capability required before doing so.

- **Assessment date:** 14 July 2026
- **Accountable owner:** Founder
- **Framework:** ODS 1.0
- **Evidence level:** Repository and operational records; no customer interview or payment evidence
- **Scope:** First individual executive customer; no enterprise sale or public launch
- **Decision:** RETURN TO PRODUCT DEVELOPMENT

## Executive Conclusion

Orendalis has a coherent product vision, isolated staging, accepted founder workflow, explainable Atlas foundation, invitation controls, and strong privacy principles. It is not revenue ready.

The company has not yet validated willingness to pay or recurring executive value with real design partners. Authentication email delivery is not accepted, recovery and monitoring exercises remain incomplete, legal and privacy materials are not approved, pricing is only principled rather than tested, and no billing or customer-support operating system is ready for a paid obligation.

Charging now would convert known learning and operational gaps into customer commitments. The correct commercial sequence is: close safety gates, validate value with design partners, define a founder-led paid offer, establish lawful billing and support, then accept one controlled payment.

## Evidence Reviewed

- Release 0.8 executive-experience reports and final `READY FOR DESIGN PARTNERS` decision;
- Release 0.9 Definition of Ready, operational-readiness report, design-partner qualification, success metrics, and exit criteria;
- founder-acceptance rerun, Atlas isolation, Workspace isolation, and regression records;
- authoritative environment, provider, integration, subscription, and billing registers;
- staging email acceptance and Microsoft support records;
- current pricing principles, ideal-customer profile, website copy, and beta support process;
- ODS Product, Work Lifecycle, Founder Approval, Security and Privacy, Documentation, Decision, and Evidence protocols.

## Verified Facts, Assumptions, and Unknowns

**Verified:** isolated staging and founder acceptance passed; Release 0.8 polish was accepted; the first ten design partners have not been operated; unassisted authentication-email delivery has not passed; restore and external monitoring evidence are absent; current pricing documentation deliberately contains no price point; no customer billing system or paid-customer operating record is evidenced.

**Assumption:** the first commercial customer is an individual senior executive served through a controlled founder-led engagement. Enterprise procurement, employer sponsorship, and public self-service commerce are outside this assessment.

**Unknown:** willingness to pay, acceptable price range, preferred billing period, recurring-use frequency, acquisition cost, support cost, renewal likelihood, target legal jurisdiction, tax treatment, and the final commercial entity or merchant arrangement. No numeric commercial forecast is inferred from these unknowns.

## Severity and Effort Scale

- **Critical:** payment or customer data must not be accepted until resolved.
- **High:** a paid customer could be acquired, but trust, delivery, or retention would be materially at risk.
- **Medium:** reduces conversion, efficiency, clarity, or measurement but has a safe manual fallback.
- **Low:** polish that can follow the first controlled paid engagement.

Effort is directional founder/team effort, not a delivery commitment: `S` up to 3 focused days, `M` 1–2 weeks, `L` 3–6 weeks, `XL` more than 6 weeks or dependent on external evidence. Legal, tax, and provider work may exceed engineering effort.

Explicit founder approval is required before legal engagement, pricing commitment, customer outreach, publishing commercial materials, introducing a billing provider, accepting terms, taking payment, inviting a design partner, or collecting new customer data.

## Final Decision

**C. RETURN TO PRODUCT DEVELOPMENT**

This is not a judgment that Orendalis lacks commercial potential. It means product value and recurring use have not yet been validated with real executives, while several Critical trust and operating gates remain open. The next product-development phase is design-partner evidence and commercial discovery—not feature expansion.
