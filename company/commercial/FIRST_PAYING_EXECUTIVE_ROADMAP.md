# First Paying Executive Roadmap

> Purpose: Define the shortest responsible path from current staging readiness to one satisfied, lawfully paying executive customer.

- **Accountable owner:** Founder
- **Objective:** Acquire and serve the first paying executive
- **Non-goals:** Public launch, enterprise sales, growth automation, feature expansion, or payment-scale architecture
- **Status:** Proposed sequence; each phase requires its own evidence and approval

## Operating Principle

The first payment is not the first proof of value. Orendalis must first show that a qualified executive reaches meaningful value, trusts Atlas, returns voluntarily, and can be supported safely. Payment then validates the offer—not the existence of the problem.

## Phase 0 — Close the Safety Gate

**Outcome:** Release 0.9 is ready for controlled design partners.

- pass unassisted authentication-email delivery;
- rehearse backup restore and rollback;
- configure and test minimal monitoring and founder notification;
- verify critical-provider recovery and billing ownership;
- reconcile migration history;
- approve design-partner privacy, consent, support, and data boundaries.

**Exit:** Every Release 0.9 Definition of Ready gate is complete and the founder explicitly approves invitations.

**Effort:** M–L. **Business impact:** Critical.

## Phase 1 — Prove Executive Value

**Outcome:** Qualified design partners can complete and understand the journey.

- recruit only against the approved qualification criteria;
- operate the 2-partner first wave before expanding;
- observe onboarding, Blueprint, Opportunity, Atlas, Decision, Ledger, and return behavior;
- capture trust, friction, time-to-value, decision usefulness, and support demand;
- correct only verified blockers through ODS-controlled releases.

**Exit:** No Critical trust or data issue, core journey succeeds without founder interpretation, and executives describe a concrete decision benefit.

**Effort:** L. **Business impact:** Critical.

## Phase 2 — Prove Recurring Value

**Outcome:** Executives return for a second meaningful career decision or operating review.

- test a calm weekly or milestone-driven executive rhythm;
- compare first-session promise with observed follow-through;
- determine which Atlas briefing, decision, or preparation moment causes return;
- record disengagement reasons without manipulative re-engagement;
- validate founder support capacity.

**Exit:** Repeat use is observed across more than one qualified executive, and the recurring value proposition can be stated without future features.

**Effort:** L–XL because elapsed time is required. **Business impact:** Critical.

## Phase 3 — Discover the Paid Offer

**Outcome:** One clear offer matches a proven executive outcome.

- conduct pricing and purchase-framing interviews;
- choose a concierge pilot or individual subscription based on evidence;
- define outcome, duration, included support, data boundary, limitations, price, currency, renewal, cancellation, and refund policy;
- test the offer verbally before building billing automation;
- record willingness to pay separately from compliments or interest.

**Exit:** A qualified executive understands the offer and expresses credible purchase intent at the proposed terms.

**Effort:** M. **Business impact:** Critical.

## Phase 4 — Establish the Commercial Trust Boundary

**Outcome:** Orendalis can form and support one paid customer relationship responsibly.

- obtain qualified legal and tax review;
- approve Terms, Privacy Notice, consent, retention, subprocessors, data rights, and account closure;
- define support promise, incident communication, continuity, and founder absence;
- publish an accurate private commercial briefing and trust statement;
- define paid-customer and contract records without storing unnecessary personal data.

**Exit:** Legal, privacy, support, security, and commercial records are approved and versioned.

**Effort:** L–XL. **Business impact:** Critical.

## Phase 5 — Prepare One Controlled Payment

**Outcome:** The company can accept, reconcile, refund, and end one payment safely.

- select the simplest compliant payment or invoice path;
- complete the founder approval brief for cost, terms, permissions, data, cancellation, and rollback;
- test invoice/receipt, payment confirmation, entitlement, failed payment, cancellation, refund, and monthly reconciliation;
- define the minimum post-payment scorecard;
- keep manual founder review for every commercial event.

**Exit:** A full fictional or provider-supported test passes without exposing credentials or creating an unintended charge.

**Effort:** M–L. **Business impact:** Critical.

## Phase 6 — Acquire the First Paying Executive

**Outcome:** One qualified executive knowingly purchases the accepted offer and reaches value.

1. Founder approves the candidate and current offer.
2. Customer receives the current commercial, legal, privacy, and support materials.
3. Customer knowingly accepts the correct versions.
4. Payment is completed through the approved path.
5. Access and entitlement are verified.
6. Assisted onboarding reaches the defined first-value milestone.
7. Support and reliability are observed during the initial period.
8. Founder reviews value, trust, cost to serve, and continuation before renewal.

**Exit:** Payment is reconciled, the customer reaches first value, no Critical issue remains, and the relationship is either renewed, completed, refunded, or closed according to the agreed terms.

**Effort:** M plus customer timing. **Business impact:** Objective achieved.

## Minimum First-Customer Scorecard

| Dimension | Evidence |
| --- | --- |
| Commercial | Offer/version accepted, amount/currency/tax state, payment and refund status |
| Activation | Invitation, verification, onboarding, first Blueprint, first meaningful Atlas decision |
| Value | Decision usefulness, time saved, confidence improvement, executive outcome statement |
| Trust | Evidence clarity, privacy comprehension, confidence in Atlas, willingness to continue |
| Reliability | Access, email, incidents, response and recovery |
| Retention | Meaningful return, renewal/cancellation decision and reason |
| Economics | Gross payment, fees, refund, net revenue, founder support time, acquisition effort |
| Rights | Consent version, export/deletion/closure requests and completion state |

## Stop Conditions

Stop acquisition or payment preparation if any Critical security, privacy, isolation, data-loss, legal, billing, recovery, access, or misleading-claim issue appears. Pause expansion when support capacity or trust evidence is inadequate. Do not compensate for weak value evidence with discounts, urgency, automation, or broader marketing.

## Roadmap Decision

The shortest responsible route to revenue is a founder-led, evidence-backed first paid engagement after design-partner validation. Building public billing, growth tooling, engagement automation, or new product modules before that evidence would increase cost without materially increasing the probability of acquiring the right first customer.
