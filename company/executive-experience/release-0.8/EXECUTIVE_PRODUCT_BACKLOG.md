# Executive Product Backlog

Purpose: Prioritize only the experience improvements that materially affect executive adoption, trust, or willingness to pay.

Ratings use High, Medium, or Low. Development effort is a relative product estimate, not a delivery commitment.

| Rank | Recommendation | Business impact | User delight | Trust improvement | Development effort |
| ---: | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Make the dashboard state-aware and center the current decision, outstanding questions, and next action | High | High | High | Medium |
| 2 | Reduce primary navigation to a small executive hierarchy; move secondary and internal destinations out of the main path | High | High | Medium | Medium |
| 3 | Replace internal release, beta, demonstration, staging, and system language on executive-facing screens | High | Medium | High | Low |
| 4 | Turn Company research into a focused dossier for the active opportunity, or show a precise honest unavailable state | High | High | High | Medium |
| 5 | Convert Atlas’s open questions into a prioritized executive action plan | High | High | High | Medium |
| 6 | Expand the Opportunity summary with confirmed facts, unknowns, compensation, source, and decision rationale already held by the Workspace | High | High | Medium | Medium |
| 7 | Make Tasks operational with priority, due date, status, and relationship to the decision | Medium | High | Medium | Medium |
| 8 | Make Career Replay useful by presenting the decision, evidence, alternatives, and outcome as a concise narrative | Medium | High | High | Medium |
| 9 | Standardize naming: Orendalis is the product, Atlas is the intelligence; remove “AI Assistant” | Medium | Medium | Medium | Low |
| 10 | Replace the long completed workflow with a concise executive decision summary and optional detail expansion | Medium | High | Medium | Medium |
| 11 | Remove duplicate breadcrumb rendering from Beta Workflow | Low | Low | Low | Low |
| 12 | Move plan demonstrations and planned entitlements out of Settings until they are relevant to a client decision | Low | Medium | Medium | Low |
| 13 | Reconcile stale operational statements in founder-only Company Control | Medium | Low | High | Low |

## Not Recommended for This Release

- new modules;
- broader automation;
- additional architecture;
- generic AI chat;
- more navigation destinations;
- additional dashboards without a clear executive decision purpose.

## Payment Decision

**If I were personally a Fortune 500 executive, I would not pay for Orendalis today.**

I would participate as a confidential design partner because the evidence discipline, uncertainty model, decision preservation, and executive agency are unusually strong.

I would not pay yet because the return dashboard is inconsistent with my completed work, Company research does not deliver intelligence, navigation requires too much product knowledge, and several screens still feel explicitly unfinished.

I would be convinced by:

1. a return experience that immediately tells me what changed and what deserves attention;
2. a useful company and opportunity intelligence dossier;
3. Atlas converting evidence gaps into a short, prioritized action plan;
4. a simpler navigation model;
5. consistently client-ready language across every executive-facing screen; and
6. evidence from several confidential executive journeys showing repeated weekly value.
