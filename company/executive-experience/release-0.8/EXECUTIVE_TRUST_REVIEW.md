# Executive Trust Review

Purpose: Assess whether Orendalis earns the level of trust required for confidential executive career decisions.

## Trust Assessment

| Dimension | Score | Evidence |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Confidentiality posture | 8/10 | Private Workspace language and isolation are explicit |
| Evidence transparency | 9/10 | Atlas identifies confirmed sources and open questions |
| Uncertainty discipline | 9/10 | Confidence is not presented as certainty |
| Executive agency | 9/10 | Atlas recommends; the executive decides |
| Product memory | 6/10 | Persistence works, but the dashboard does not reflect completion |
| Information consistency | 6/10 | Module summaries are consistent after remediation; internal screens still contain stale statements |
| Professional readiness | 6/10 | Strong core flow, visible beta language and unfinished research surfaces |
| Overall trust | 7/10 | Trustworthy decision principles inside an incomplete product experience |

## Trust Strengths

- Atlas does not fabricate missing information.
- Evidence sources are visible and understandable.
- The recommendation explains what would change it.
- The executive’s decision is clearly separated from Atlas’s recommendation.
- Immutable records and replay protection reinforce permanence.
- Demonstration data is explicitly isolated from the founder Workspace.

## Trust Risks

### High

- The stale dashboard contradicts the promise of durable memory.
- Company research promises intelligence that the page does not provide.
- Internal release and staging language signals that the experience is not yet client-ready.

### Medium

- Company Control contains stale operational statements. Although founder-only, inaccurate control information weakens governance trust.
- “AI Assistant” understates and confuses the Atlas identity.
- The product does not yet explain how an executive can inspect, correct, or challenge an individual confirmed record from the module pages.

## Would I Entrust My Career Data?

For a confidential, supervised design-partner engagement: yes, provided the founder explains the staging boundary and data-handling model.

For autonomous daily use without direct support: not yet. The product needs consistent state awareness, client-ready language, and useful module depth before the executive can trust every surface equally.

## What Would Increase Trust Most

1. Make the dashboard reflect the latest confirmed decision and next action.
2. Remove internal beta, release, demonstration, and staging language from executive-facing screens.
3. Make Company research and Opportunity intelligence substantively useful or clearly unavailable.
4. Provide a concise “why this is here” and “what Atlas used” view on every record summary.
5. Align all product naming around Atlas and Orendalis.
