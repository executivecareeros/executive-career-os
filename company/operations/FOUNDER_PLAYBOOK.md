# Founder Playbook

> Purpose: Define recurring founder responsibilities so essential company work does not depend on memory or urgency.

## Daily

- Confirm the highest-value company outcome and the day's operating constraints.
- Review production, security, customer, and financial alerts requiring action.
- Protect focused product and engineering time.
- Record material decisions, customer insights, incidents, and commitments.
- Avoid irreversible actions without evidence, rollback, and approval discipline.

## Weekly

- Review product priorities, engineering progress, quality, and technical debt.
- Review customer conversations, feedback, support, and unresolved promises.
- Review cash commitments, upcoming payments, and material vendor changes.
- Review infrastructure and security health, access, backups, and incidents.
- Review legal, privacy, and compliance actions due.
- Review hiring needs before creating roles or commitments.
- Review marketing claims for accuracy and alignment with product reality.
- Assess company health: focus, pace, risk, energy, and unresolved decisions.

## Monthly

- Complete `MONTH_END_CHECKLIST.md` and `COMPANY_SCORECARD.md`.
- Update the Founder Investment Ledger and recurring-expense records.
- Reconcile roadmap, release posture, customer evidence, and documentation.
- Review Microsoft 365, GitHub, Supabase, Vercel, domain, DNS, and security controls.
- Review contracts, renewals, legal deadlines, access ownership, and recovery readiness.
- Decide which work to stop, continue, or escalate.

## Quarterly

- Complete `QUARTERLY_REVIEW.md`.
- Revalidate strategy, product direction, architecture, runway, market assumptions, and risks.
- Review customer concentration, beta evidence, pricing hypotheses, and competitive change.
- Review security posture, vendor dependence, founder dependence, and business continuity.
- Assess hiring, advisers, professional services, and delegation needs.

## Yearly

- Complete `YEARLY_REVIEW.md`.
- Reaffirm or revise mission, vision, principles, and long-term company goals.
- Review financial records with qualified professionals.
- Review legal structure, intellectual property, insurance, privacy, and regulatory obligations.
- Conduct access, recovery, continuity, and incident-response exercises.
- Preserve founder reflections, lessons, milestones, and the approved annual plan.

## Responsibility Map

| Area | Founder obligation |
| --- | --- |
| Engineering | Protect quality, architecture, security, testability, and sustainable delivery. |
| Product | Keep executive decision value and customer evidence central. |
| Customers | Listen directly, protect trust, and close commitments. |
| Finance | Preserve records, control commitments, monitor runway, and seek professional advice. |
| Legal | Track obligations and obtain qualified counsel for material decisions. |
| Infrastructure | Maintain ownership, resilience, observability, rollback, and recovery. |
| Security | Minimize access, protect secrets, review incidents, and verify controls. |
| Hiring | Hire for defined outcomes with explicit authority and accountability. |
| Marketing | Make only accurate, supportable claims. |
| Company Health | Monitor focus, pace, resilience, culture, and founder capacity. |
