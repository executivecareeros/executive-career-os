# Orendalis Operations

> Purpose: Define the permanent operating practices used to run Orendalis responsibly, consistently, and with durable institutional memory.

## What Operations Owns

Operations turns company intent into recurring disciplines: decision rights, review cadences, controls, risk management, incident response, change approval, company health, and evidence preservation. It provides the routines by which commitments are reviewed and work is made accountable.

Operations differs from:

- **Engineering**, which designs, builds, tests, deploys, and maintains technical systems.
- **Company History**, which preserves what happened, why it mattered, and the evidence of the company's evolution.
- **Product Documentation**, which defines product behavior, user value, requirements, releases, and product decisions.

Operations may reference these areas but does not replace their authoritative records.

## Ownership

The founder owns Operations until responsibility is formally delegated. Every operational area must have one accountable owner, a review date, and an evidence location. Delegation transfers execution authority only within documented limits; it does not remove founder or board accountability.

## Review Cadence

- Weekly: priorities, commitments, customers, incidents, and immediate risks.
- Monthly: scorecard, expenses, subscriptions, security, vendors, roadmap, and documentation.
- Quarterly: strategy, architecture, runway, risks, customers, and operating effectiveness.
- Annually: mission, governance, financial position, resilience, lessons, and goals.
- After material events: incidents, major changes, financing, legal changes, releases, and vendor migrations.

## Operating Records

This folder contains the company operating system, founder routines, review templates, incident and change controls, risk register, company scorecard, readiness gates, and service-specific operational records such as `email/`.
