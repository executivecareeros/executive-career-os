# Luna–Sol Escalation Policy

**Status:** Active under ODS 2.0  
**Owner:** Founder / Operations  
**Scope:** Internal execution, product, reliability, security, and release work

## Operating rule

Luna is the default model for routine, bounded work. Sol is used when deeper reasoning or broader system capability materially improves a live-product, inventory, ranking, Atlas, security, privacy, activation, applications, or revenue outcome.

Every unresolved issue ends in exactly one state: **Fixed by Luna**, **Recorded for Sol**, or **Immediate Sol Escalation Required**. No issue may be silently dropped or retried without materially new evidence.

## Severity

- **P0 — Immediate Sol escalation:** a core journey is blocked, data integrity/security/privacy may be at risk, production is unavailable, or two evidence-based Luna repairs have failed on the same blocker.
- **P1 — Sol within 24 hours:** material CV, ranking, inventory, Atlas, mobile, or cross-system defects that survive two targeted Luna attempts.
- **P2 — Record for Sol batch:** architecture, redesign, provider research, scaling, or other valuable non-blocking work.
- **P3 — Luna backlog:** low-impact polish or optional work without measured value.

For P0, stop lower-priority work, preserve evidence, register the issue, notify the Founder with **“Switch to Sol now.”**, and provide one complete Sol mission. P1 permits one final bounded Luna attempt only when a new concrete hypothesis exists. P2 issues are reviewed weekly; P3 remains with Luna.

## Attempt limit

For one unresolved issue, Luna may perform one evidence-gathering pass, two targeted repairs, and one live validation after each repair. A further attempt requires new evidence such as a new log, trace, schema state, reproduction path, integration, or materially different hypothesis.

## Sol mission minimum

Every Sol handoff includes objective, verified facts, environment, reproduction, evidence, commits, schemas/routes, Luna attempts and failure reason, constraints, required systems, acceptance tests, live validation, rollback conditions, and return format.

## Notification and review

Immediate Founder notification is required for P0, credentials/MFA, payment, legal acceptance, critical security/privacy issues, or irreversible actions. Review all open Sol issues every seven days; close obsolete issues, merge true duplicates, re-score risk, and select at most one focused batch of three related issues.

This policy does not authorize spending, legal acceptance, identity transfer, or access to secrets.
