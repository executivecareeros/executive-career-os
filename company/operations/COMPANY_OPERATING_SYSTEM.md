# Company Operating System

> Purpose: Establish how Orendalis converts its mission into decisions, responsibilities, reviews, and accountable execution.

## Mission

Build the professional operating system that helps people preserve career history, understand patterns, and make consequential decisions with evidence, clarity, and control.

## Vision

Atlas becomes a trusted chief of staff for professional life while the executive remains the final decision-maker and owner of their career and data.

## Values

- Evidence before opinion.
- Memory before intelligence.
- Clarity before complexity.
- Trust before automation.
- Human judgment before system action.
- Stewardship, portability, and durable history.
- Honest uncertainty and accountable decisions.

## Decision-Making Principles

1. Identify the decision owner and affected stakeholders.
2. Separate facts, assumptions, risks, alternatives, and unknowns.
3. Prefer reversible experiments when evidence is incomplete.
4. Require explicit approval for irreversible, costly, public, security-sensitive, or ownership-changing actions.
5. Record material decisions, reasoning, evidence, and review conditions.
6. Do not trade privacy, security, or truthfulness for speed.
7. Revisit decisions when their assumptions materially change.

## Responsibilities

### Founder

The founder protects mission, capital, ownership, trust, and long-term coherence; appoints decision owners; approves material commitments; maintains financial and legal oversight; and ensures that company memory survives individual context.

### Executives and Functional Owners

Executives translate strategy into measurable commitments, surface risks early, preserve decision evidence, operate within approved authority, and report outcomes without hiding uncertainty. Each owner is accountable for controls, succession, documentation, and escalation in their area.

## Operating Cadence

| Cadence | Focus | Output |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Daily | Priorities, customers, service health, blockers | Updated actions and escalations |
| Weekly | Commitments, product, engineering, customer learning, cash, risks | Weekly decisions and owners |
| Monthly | Scorecard, finances, vendors, security, roadmap, documentation | Month-end record and corrective actions |
| Quarterly | Strategy, architecture, runway, market, risk, organization | Quarterly review and revised priorities |
| Annual | Mission, performance, resilience, governance, lessons, goals | Annual company record and approved plan |

## Meeting Cadence

Meet only when synchronous judgment is required. Every recurring meeting has an owner, written agenda, decision purpose, pre-read, time limit, actions, and decision record. Cancel meetings that have no decision or coordination need.

For a single-founder company, these meetings are structured written reviews. As the team grows, participants are limited to decision owners and essential contributors.

## Review Standards

Monthly, quarterly, and annual reviews must distinguish measured results from estimates, list unresolved risks, name accountable owners, and preserve decisions in the appropriate company record. Reviews are complete only when actions have owners and dates.
