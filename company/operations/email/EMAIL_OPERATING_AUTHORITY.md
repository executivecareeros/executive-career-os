## Delegated Actions

Within the mandate, Executive Operations may prepare, draft, send, and organize lawful and relevant company email; reply to ordinary business messages; maintain approved templates; conduct low-volume personalized outreach; follow up with explicitly consented users; and answer routine product or customer-success questions.

This authority does not extend to personal Founder statements, binding commitments, contracts, legal acceptance, financial commitments, or messages that require undisclosed personal judgment. Those messages must be prepared as drafts for Founder review.

## Safety Boundaries

The following remain prohibited:

- bulk unsolicited email or purchased-list campaigns;
- deceptive messages, fabricated customer claims, or guaranteed-employment claims;
- confidential user information, CV details, or sensitive career information without explicit authorization;
- impersonating the Founder personally;
- binding commercial offers that have not been approved;
- storing passwords, MFA codes, tokens, cookies, recovery codes, or message contents in the repository;
- creating forwarding, delegation, application passwords, API permissions, or administrator privileges without a separate necessity and approval review.

Before outreach, verify recipient relevance and a lawful communication basis, personalize the message, include a clear opt-out where appropriate, limit volume, and monitor bounces and complaints. Stop sending if deliverability or complaint risk rises.

## Authentication

Use Microsoft 365's secure authenticated session. The Founder enters passwords and MFA personally when required. Prefer delegated provider-managed access where available; never request or record credentials.

## Audit Standard

Record material configuration changes and aggregate communication outcomes. Do not record credentials, private message bodies, recipient career data, or other unnecessary personal information.
