# First Impression Report

> Purpose: Capture what a senior executive is likely to believe and feel during the first authenticated encounter with Orendalis.

## First Five Minutes

The initial visual impression is premium, calm, and deliberate. The dark palette, generous typography, restrained status colors, and clear dashboard call to action create confidence. The promise of a persistent Career Memory and an evidence-led Atlas is distinctive.

Confidence falls quickly when the primary action leads to `Workflow unavailable`. Exploring alternative modules then reveals a fictional Workspace, a fully completed fictional Blueprint, scored opportunities, urgent tasks, and a populated Career Archive. Although demo notices are present, these records occupy the same surfaces and visual hierarchy as personal career data.

## Likely Executive Questions

- Is this my workspace or a product demonstration?
- Did Atlas create conclusions about me before I provided information?
- Which recommendation is based on my evidence?
- Why does the dashboard ask me to continue a journey I cannot access?
- Why are there more than twenty modules before I have completed my profile?
- Is Company Control current if it says staging does not exist while I am using staging?

## Trust-Creating Moments

- Secure, professional login language.
- Clear return-path behavior after logout.
- Privacy and consent language before history import.
- Explicit confirmation before information becomes Career Memory.
- Append-only correction model.
- Atlas acknowledgement of uncertainty and missing evidence.

## Trust-Reducing Moments

- Unavailable core journey.
- Successful return login sending a completed founder back to blank onboarding.
- Fictional career records appearing in a live personal workspace.
- Stale operational status in Company Control and Invitation Management.
- Technical identifiers and rule codes in executive recommendations.
- Slow transitions without visible progress.
- Tablet clipping of invitation controls.

## First-Impression Verdict

The design earns attention, but the data-state contradictions prevent trust. A senior executive is likely to admire the ambition and then hesitate to provide sensitive career information until the product proves that it distinguishes personal truth, fictional examples, and system diagnostics without ambiguity.
