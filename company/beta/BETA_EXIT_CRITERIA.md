# Private Beta Exit Criteria

> Purpose: Define when Orendalis may conclude, pause, extend, or progress beyond the initial design-partner beta.

## Successful Completion

- The approved cohort completes enough observed journeys to evaluate decision clarity.
- No unresolved Critical issue remains.
- High issues have accepted remediation or explicit stop decisions.
- Participants can explain why Atlas reached its assessment and what remains unknown.
- Evidence inspection, disagreement, and trust concerns are understood.
- Data lifecycle requests are completed correctly.
- Support, monitoring, restore, and incident processes operate as accepted.
- Founder records lessons and a release decision without extrapolating from the tiny cohort.

## Pause Criteria

Pause immediately for cross-workspace access, secret exposure, destructive history mutation, failed recovery, misleading assessment claims, inability to honor lifecycle requests, or material legal/privacy uncertainty.

## Extension

Extend only to answer a specific unresolved learning question. Do not expand product scope or cohort size by default.

## Progression

Completion does not authorize public beta, billing, automation, or real-data expansion. Each requires a separate release and evidence gate.
