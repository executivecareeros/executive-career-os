# Project Simplicity

## Experience rule

Executives use familiar job-search behaviour; Atlas carries the complexity. The visible product starts with three actions: upload a CV, search executive jobs, and review recommendations. Internal models—including Blueprint, canonical opportunities, reasoning, and the Career Ledger—remain intact but are not navigation concepts.

## Current experience

- The public entry is white, quiet, and action-led.
- Onboarding asks only how the executive wants to begin: upload a CV or skip to jobs.
- Jobs provides two first-class choices: **Search jobs** and **Atlas recommendations**.
- Search uses conventional role, location, company, industry, work-model, and salary controls.
- Authenticated navigation is limited to Home, Jobs, Companies, Applications, and Profile.
- Atlas is presented as an available second perspective, not a mandatory report or workflow.

## Guardrails

- No intelligence architecture was removed or weakened.
- No recommendation is fabricated when context is absent.
- Profile completion remains progressive.
- Empty states state what is unavailable and give one useful next action.
- Provider collection remains available, but is secondary to executive search.

## Acceptance standard

A first-time executive should identify where to upload a CV, where to search, and where to find Atlas recommendations in under 60 seconds without documentation or training.
