# Institutional Learning

Last updated: 2026-07-17 · Owner: Sol / Governance Owner · Version: `orion-institutional-learning-v1`

## Purpose

Orion Institutional Learning preserves reviewed knowledge about Orion's product, validation, governance, and engineering processes. It does not create facts about executives or employers and does not adapt models automatically.

## Canonical domains

The registry covers validated decision patterns, recurring evidence gaps, frequently requested evidence, recommendation rejection and acceptance reasons, explanation effectiveness, confidence calibration findings, validation process improvements, engineering process improvements, and governance decisions.

Every record states an observation, reviewed supporting evidence and provenance, documented reasoning, approval history, scope, confidence, reuse targets, and a review or expiration requirement.

## Boundary

Only the latest `Approved`, unexpired, not-due revision may inform documentation, review playbooks, validation procedures, evidence requirements, decision guidance, or engineering practices. Historical recommendations, outcomes, evidence, and learning revisions remain unchanged. Draft, under-review, superseded, retired, archived, expired, and review-due records cannot influence current guidance.

Institutional evidence describes Orion. Employer and executive assertions remain in their existing evidence and Knowledge Graph systems.

## Current evidence

The deterministic fixture validates one approved recurring-evidence-gap record and the complete governance contract. Its 10% domain coverage, one approved record, and one evidence-gap opportunity are fixture results only. Live institutional-learning coverage remains Unknown until reviewed production evidence is entered through an approved persistence surface.
