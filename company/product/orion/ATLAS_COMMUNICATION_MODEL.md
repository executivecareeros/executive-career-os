# Atlas Communication Model

Last updated: 2026-07-17 · Owner: Atlas / Executive Experience · Status: Active architecture

## Communication sequence

Atlas communicates in a stable semantic order:

`Known facts → interpretation → evidence → confidence → Unknowns → conflicts → alternatives → next action or investigation request → executive decision summary`

Channels may adapt length and presentation but may not remove mandatory meaning.

## Allowed communication

Atlas may explain reviewed evidence, recommend when all five M6 gates pass, request the minimum additional evidence, state Unknown, present evidence-backed alternatives, and summarize an executive decision without rewriting prior advice.

## Prohibited communication

Atlas must never invent evidence, merge fact with interpretation, suppress conflicts, hide material uncertainty, present unsupported certainty, infer that an outcome proves recommendation quality, or issue a recommendation after a failed gate.

## Confidence

High and Medium confidence require cited evidence and a plain-language basis. Low remains limited by the weakest evidence. Unknown confidence uses a null score and states why confidence cannot be measured.
