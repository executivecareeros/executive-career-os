# Orion Strategy

Status: Active strategy · Owner: Sol · Product owner: Founder

## Purpose

ORENDALIS exists to become the single trusted place where executives discover, understand, compare, and act on executive opportunities. The product wins by turning attributable market observations into decision-ready intelligence, not by reproducing a generic job board.

## Value and differentiation

- Executives receive one canonical opportunity, its provenance, freshness, uncertainty, and an explainable Atlas assessment instead of duplicate listings.
- Employers ultimately gain a verified company presence and high-quality executive demand intelligence, but never control independent ranking truth.
- Opportunity intelligence explains role, geography, compensation, eligibility, and evidence. Employer intelligence explains identity, structure, hiring activity, confidence, and recency.
- Quality outranks raw volume. Unknown stays unknown. Inference is labeled. Source observations remain attributable.

## Network sequence

1. Build broad, lawful provider coverage through the common Coverage Engine.
2. Verify canonical employers and enrich opportunity records.
3. Improve search and Atlas from canonical evidence.
4. After M2, permit employer claims with domain and representative verification.
5. After claim governance is stable and M3 readiness is established, permit publishing through the same validation pipeline.

This sequencing creates executive-side utility before monetizing the employer side. Revenue can evolve from executive subscription and design partnerships to verified employer analytics, governed publishing, and partner/API products. Payment can never override recommendation quality.

## Trust rules

- Preserve source URL, provider, original identifier, observed time, and confidence.
- Never fabricate compensation, location, remote scope, employer facts, or relationships.
- Never show closed evidence as active; retain history rather than erase it.
- Never allow an employer claim to remove provenance, rewrite history, or manipulate Atlas.
- Separate network coverage from any executive profile. Profiles affect ranking, never inventory metrics.

## Five-year direction

Orion progresses from a measured opportunity network (M1), to rich decision-ready intelligence (M2), to a governed two-sided platform (M3), and finally to a global, API-ready executive opportunity knowledge system with defensible history and market signals (M4).

## Anti-goals

- Becoming a generic job board.
- Accepting unverified, low-quality listings as fact.
- Allowing paid placement to override recommendation quality.
- Allowing employers to bypass validation and the intelligence pipeline.
- Fabricating missing company or opportunity information.
- Hiding freshness, confidence, provenance, or uncertainty.
- Optimizing vanity metrics over executive outcomes.
- Scraping sources without a lawful and operationally supportable basis.
