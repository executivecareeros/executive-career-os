# Disclosure Rules

Last updated: 2026-07-17 · Owner: Executive Experience / Governance · Status: Binding architecture

## Always visible when material

- the facts Orion is using;
- the evidence supporting those facts;
- Orion's interpretation, visibly separated from fact;
- confidence level and basis;
- known evidence gaps and their decision impact;
- unresolved conflicting claims;
- credible evidence-backed alternatives;
- whether a decision gate failed;
- the minimum justified next action or investigation request.

## Never hidden

Material uncertainty, contrary evidence, limitations, stale or incomplete evidence, failed decision gates, and unresolved conflicts cannot be removed for brevity or presentation convenience.

## Never disclosed as fact

Assumptions, unreviewed free text, unsupported alternatives, invented evidence, causal claims inferred from outcomes, and confidence unsupported by evidence cannot be presented as established truth.

## Channel rule

A channel may progressively disclose detail, but the executive must be able to reach every mandatory disclosure within the same interaction. Summaries may shorten language; they may not change semantic status or confidence.
