# Executive Rooms Moderation and Appeal Runbook

## Principles

Safety actions are proportionate, evidence-preserving, append-only, time-stamped and appeal-capable. Owners and moderators enforce room rules; platform administrators act only for platform safety, legal obligations or account compromise.

## Intake

1. Record report category without copying unnecessary confidential content.
2. Preserve the minimum evidence reference and content hash.
3. Acknowledge receipt without promising an outcome.
4. Triage severity: imminent harm/account compromise; severe harassment/privacy breach; ordinary conduct; content-quality dispute.

## Actions

| Severity | Initial action | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Imminent harm or compromised account | Restrict affected account/room, preserve evidence, invoke incident protocol | Immediate |
| Privacy exposure or severe harassment | Hide content from participants, suspend posting, notify platform safety owner | Same business day |
| Ordinary conduct violation | Moderator warning, mute or remove with stated reason | Two business days |
| Evidence/quality disagreement | Label disputed, request source, do not treat as misconduct | Three business days |

Permanent deletion is not the first moderation action when evidence must be preserved. Participant visibility and security retention are separate states.

## Appeal

The affected participant receives action type, reason category, time, duration and appeal route. A reviewer who did not issue the original action evaluates the appeal. Outcome is appended; original history is not rewritten.

## Atlas

Atlas cannot moderate, suspend or remove. It may summarize a moderator-selected evidence set only after explicit invocation, and its output is advisory and labelled as interpretation.

## Metrics

Reports by category; acknowledgement time; resolution time; appeals; reversals; repeat violations; unauthorized-access attempts; moderator workload. Never optimize engagement or publicize user-level moderation statistics.
