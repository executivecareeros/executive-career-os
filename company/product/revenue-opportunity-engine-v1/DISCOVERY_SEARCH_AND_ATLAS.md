# Discovery, Search, Blueprint, and Atlas

> Purpose: Define the executive experience after opportunities enter the normalized universe.

- **Authority:** ODS 1.0 Product track
- **Status:** Version 1 architecture complete
- **Date:** 14 July 2026
- **Scope:** Discovery, search, Blueprint matching, and explainable Atlas recommendations; no implementation

## One Universe, Two Entry Modes

Discovery and Search use the same accepted Opportunity revisions, duplicate clusters, Company identities, provenance, and freshness. They are not separate inventories.

### Discovery — Default

Discovery answers: **What deserves my attention without requiring me to search?**

It presents a calm, finite briefing organized by:

- newly relevant;
- materially changed;
- closing or stale;
- strong Blueprint alignment;
- important conflict or unknown;
- executive-imported and awaiting review.

Discovery is generated through deterministic filters and transparent ranking. It does not claim that absence means no opportunity exists.

### Search — Available

Search answers: **What can I find in the current Orendalis universe?**

Version 1 supports lexical and faceted retrieval across title, function, company, country, city, remote status, industry, executive level, opportunity type, source, freshness, compensation presence, and lifecycle. Results expose source and last verification.

Search does not bypass Blueprint constraints; it may show broader results, but it clearly distinguishes search relevance from executive fit.

## Blueprint Matching

### Hard Constraints

Hard constraints may exclude or clearly flag an Opportunity only when the active Blueprint explicitly defines them:

- excluded countries or relocation conditions;
- non-negotiable travel limits;
- minimum leadership level;
- required language;
- explicit industry or ownership exclusion;
- minimum compensation when comparable evidence exists;
- executive principles designated as non-negotiable.

Unknown data does not count as a failed match. It creates a question.

### Fit Lenses

- career direction and mandate;
- leadership scope and authority;
- industry and company context;
- geography, travel, and family constraints;
- compensation and ownership;
- Career Capital contribution;
- timing and opportunity cost;
- relationships and access;
- evidence completeness and risk.

Each lens reports `Aligned`, `Potentially Aligned`, `Conflict`, or `Unknown`, with evidence. A transparent priority band may summarize the view; no hidden universal fit score is required.

## Discovery Ranking

The ranking sequence is deterministic:

1. remove closed, revoked, ineligible, or unsupported records;
2. enforce explicit non-negotiable constraints where evidence is sufficient;
3. prioritize executive-level and mandate relevance;
4. consider Blueprint alignment by lens;
5. increase priority for credible freshness and material change;
6. decrease priority for weak provenance, staleness, or unresolved identity;
7. diversify by company and source to avoid duplicate saturation;
8. surface high-value unknowns rather than hiding incomplete opportunities;
9. preserve an explanation of every material ranking factor.

## Atlas Recommendation Flow

`Opportunity Revision → Company Briefing → Blueprint Lens Review → Career Context → Conflicts and Unknowns → Alternatives → Recommendation → Executive Decision → Snapshot`

Atlas shows:

- why the opportunity appeared;
- what changed;
- which Blueprint evidence was used;
- gains, losses, conflicts, and opportunity cost;
- what is unknown and the next best question;
- confidence in existence, assessment, and action separately;
- alternatives: review, save, monitor, network first, apply, negotiate, or dismiss;
- what would change the recommendation.

Atlas never applies, contacts, or dismisses on the executive’s behalf in Version 1.

## Feedback and Learning

Executive actions create explicit learning evidence:

- relevant or not relevant, with optional reason;
- saved or dismissed;
- company or source preference correction;
- Blueprint conflict confirmed or corrected;
- progressed to conversation or application;
- decision and eventual outcome.

Feedback adjusts future deterministic prioritization only through transparent, versioned rules or confirmed Blueprint changes. Dismissal does not silently change the Blueprint.
