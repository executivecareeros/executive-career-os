# Company Asset Management System

> Purpose: Preserve ownership, continuity, renewal, recovery, and risk information for every material company asset.

Company assets must be tracked because value can be lost through expired domains, inaccessible accounts, unclear ownership, missed renewals, compromised credentials, or undocumented dependencies. This folder is owned by the founder until a Corporate Secretary, COO, or Security Officer is formally assigned.

## Governance

- Review critical access and renewals monthly, the full register quarterly, and ownership/continuity annually.
- Add an asset when the company creates, purchases, licenses, registers, controls, or materially depends on it.
- Assign one accountable owner, status, criticality, location, recovery path, and renewal information.
- Never record passwords, secret keys, recovery codes, private keys, or sensitive personal information here.
- Retire assets through a documented decision: export records, revoke access, cancel renewal, preserve legal/audit evidence, transfer ownership where required, and mark the asset Retired rather than deleting its history.

## Ownership principles

Company assets should use founder-controlled or company-controlled accounts, company billing information, least-privilege access, MFA, documented recovery, and a backup administrator where appropriate. Personal ownership should be treated as transitional and documented. Legal ownership questions require qualified professional advice.

## Status vocabulary

Use `Active`, `Planned`, `Pending`, `Under Review`, `Suspended`, `Retiring`, or `Retired`. Add changes to the Notes column with date and decision reference.

This documentation is operational guidance, not legal, tax, insurance, or intellectual-property advice.
