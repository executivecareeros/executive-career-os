# Founder Offboarding and Asset Transfer Checklist

> Purpose: Ensure every critical company asset can be transferred lawfully and securely if founder responsibilities change.

This checklist requires executed corporate/legal authority. It does not itself authorize transfer.

- [ ] Confirm board/company authority, effective date, scope, confidentiality, and legal advice.
- [ ] Inventory company, domain, repository, hosting, database, authentication, email, payment, analytics, monitoring, legal, accounting, social, developer, hardware, license, and certificate assets.
- [ ] Name successor owners and backup administrators; verify company-controlled emails.
- [ ] Transfer roles through provider-supported processes—never share passwords.
- [ ] Rotate passwords, tokens, keys, recovery email/phone, payment methods, and support PINs as required.
- [ ] Transfer registrar control, DNS, DNSSEC, lock, renewal, certificates, and domain invoices.
- [ ] Transfer GitHub organization/repository administration and verify complete history/backups.
- [ ] Transfer Vercel/Supabase ownership, billing, support, projects, backups, logs, and incident authority.
- [ ] Transfer email super-admin, aliases, retention, recovery, and transactional sender control.
- [ ] Preserve IP assignments, founder agreements, cap table, contracts, registrations, tax/accounting, insurance, and decision logs.
- [ ] Return or securely erase company hardware/data under documented policy; preserve legal holds.
- [ ] Revoke former access only after successor access and recovery are tested.
- [ ] Conduct continuity walkthrough and sign an asset-transfer acceptance record.
- [ ] Notify vendors, team members, customers, insurers, banks, and authorities only as legally/operationally required.
- [ ] Schedule 24-hour and 30-day access audits.
