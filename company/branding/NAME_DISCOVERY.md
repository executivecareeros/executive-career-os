# Company Name Discovery

**Research date:** 12 July 2026  
**Scope:** Parent company for Executive Career OS and future products  
**Method:** brand-first linguistic generation, automatic rejection, web/common-law screening, preliminary trademark screening, and `.com` registry checks.

## What enduring technology names have in common

Apple, Google, Microsoft, OpenAI, Stripe, Notion, Figma, Linear, Atlassian, Asana, Vercel, Anthropic, Datadog, Palantir, Canva, HubSpot, Slack, Zoom, and Snowflake use several repeatable patterns:

1. **They are compact verbal objects.** Most are one word and easy to place in a sentence.
2. **They carry an image or idea, not a feature list.** Apple, Stripe, Snowflake, Palantir, and Notion create associations without trapping the company in a category.
3. **Their sound has structure.** Strong consonants provide definition; open vowels make the word speakable.
4. **They tolerate expansion.** The corporate name can sit above products that did not exist when the company was founded.
5. **They are distinctive enough to own.** Coined or unexpected dictionary terms are usually stronger marks than descriptive phrases.
6. **They pass conversation tests.** “I work at Stripe” and “the founders of Canva” sound natural.
7. **They are not all short in the same way.** Memorability and distinctiveness matter more than an arbitrary character count.

The strategic lesson is to prefer a distinctive sound-and-meaning vessel over a literal description of employment, software, or artificial intelligence.

## Automatic rejection logic

The longlist was filtered for prohibited terms, cheap/childish tone, crypto-like construction, difficult spelling, awkward sentence use, excessive category description, and obvious existing famous brands. Repetition in the generation record is intentional evidence that some sound families converged; duplicates count as rejected explorations, not unique finalists.

## Research limitation

Search engines and public databases cannot prove worldwide clearance. Absence of a result is not evidence of a legal right.

> **Legal disclaimer:** This research provides preliminary availability only. Final trademark clearance must be performed by a qualified trademark attorney before adoption. Final legal clearance is outside the scope of this research.
