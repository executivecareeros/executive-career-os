# Final Decision

## The company name is **Orendalis**

One name. One recommendation.

Orendalis wins because it has the rare balance the board was seeking: warm but not soft, intelligent but not clinical, premium but not aristocratic, distinctive but pronounceable, and expansive without becoming vague.

The new round produced attractive alternatives. None clearly surpassed it:

- **Orenalis** lost because its smoother surface has less structure, confidence, and recall.
- **Orenvera** lost because the `vera` family is familiar and crowded, reducing uniqueness.
- **Radelora** lost because it leans toward fantasy and luxury rather than trusted intelligence.
- **Valedara** lost because spelling and place-name associations weaken first-hearing ownership.
- **Noveridian** lost because it is long and engineered.
- **Talenoria** lost because it points back toward talent and an industry the parent company must outgrow.

The decisive sentence is:

> **“Welcome to the twentieth anniversary of Orendalis.”**

It sounds like a company whose reputation was earned over time. That is the standard.

## Required action before adoption

1. Obtain attorney-led trademark clearance in launch and expansion jurisdictions, particularly against Orenda-family marks in relevant classes.
2. Recheck and secure `orendalis.com` immediately after counsel’s initial approval.
3. Secure priority country domains, handles, and app-store developer identities confidentially.
4. Run native-speaker adverse-meaning and hearing-to-spelling tests.
5. Only then approve incorporation, identity design, and public announcement.
