# Founder Decision Memo

**To:** Founders  
**From:** Independent Review Board  
**Date:** 12 July 2026  
**Subject:** Final decision on Orendalis

## Decision

**APPROVE WITH MINOR CONCERNS.**

Orendalis is approved as the permanent company identity, conditional on full trademark clearance and successful acquisition of `orendalis.com`.

## What this means

The strategic naming decision is complete. The founders should stop searching and move forward with branding.

Further open-ended naming work now creates more risk than value: decision fatigue, attachment to novelty, inconsistent internal language, and possible loss of the exact domain. Reconsider the name only if one of the formal adoption gates fails.

## Why the board approved it

- It sounds natural in employment, media, product, investor, and public-company language.
- It is not tied to careers, artificial intelligence, software, or any current product.
- It is distinctive enough to become a single corporate identity.
- It balances warmth, intelligence, trust, and premium perception.
- It can credibly support a portfolio containing Executive Career OS, Atlas Intelligence, Career Passport, and future products.
- The 30-year sentence works: **“Welcome to the twentieth anniversary of Orendalis.”**

## Minor concerns accepted by the board

1. Four syllables are slightly longer than ideal, but speech remains fluid.
2. First-hearing spelling will not be perfect for every listener, but ambiguity is limited and teachable.
3. The name requires the company to create its meaning; that is appropriate for a parent brand.
4. Similar Orenda-family marks require attorney-led review. This is a gate, not a cosmetic concern.

## Formal adoption gates

Proceed only when all four are satisfied:

1. **Legal:** Qualified trademark counsel issues a favorable written clearance opinion for priority jurisdictions and relevant goods/services.
2. **Domain:** `orendalis.com` is registered in the correct company-controlled account.
3. **Language:** Native-speaker checks find no serious adverse meaning or unacceptable pronunciation problem in priority markets.
4. **Governance:** The board/founders record the corporate naming decision and approve the owner entity, brand architecture, and authorized domain administrators.

## Stop conditions

Reopen the naming decision only if:

- counsel identifies an unacceptable infringement or registrability risk;
- the exact `.com` becomes unavailable and cannot be acquired on acceptable terms before adoption;
- native-language research identifies a serious real-world problem; or
- the company’s legal structure makes ownership or use impossible.

Personal preference changes, new suggestions, and minor pronunciation variation are not stop conditions.

## Immediate authorization requested

Authorize a small confidential adoption team consisting of one founder, trademark counsel, one technical domain administrator, and one brand lead. Limit access until the domain, filings, and core handles are secured.

## Final statement

Adopt **Orendalis** subject to the stated gates. Stop searching. Begin controlled legal, domain, identity, and operational adoption.

> **Legal disclaimer:** This research provides preliminary availability only. Final trademark clearance must be performed by a qualified trademark attorney before adoption. Final legal clearance is outside the scope of this research.
