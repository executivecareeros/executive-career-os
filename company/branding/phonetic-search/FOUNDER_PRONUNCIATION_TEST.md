# Founder Pronunciation Test

## Purpose

This document provides a blind, repeatable founder test for future candidates. It is not an invitation to reconsider the rejected finalists.

## Setup

Use a neutral speaker who has not seen the spellings. Record each candidate once in the same sentence and play candidates in randomized order. Do not show logos, meanings, domains, or written forms.

## Round 1: immediate hearing

After hearing each name once, the founder should:

1. repeat it immediately;
2. write the spelling they expect;
3. rate confidence from 1 to 5;
4. say whether they would be comfortable giving it over the phone.

Reject any candidate that needs a correction, spelling hint, etymology, or second playback.

## Round 2: natural speech

Without rehearsal, say:

- “Hi, I’m Cüneyt, founder of ___.”
- “Thank you for calling ___.”
- “I work at ___.”
- “Atlas is developed by ___.”
- “Executive Career OS, by ___.”
- “___ announces its European expansion.”
- “Open ___.”
- “Connect my account to ___.”

Repeat the founder introduction ten times at normal speed. Reject hesitation, pronunciation drift, embarrassment, or the urge to explain the name.

## Round 3: delayed recall

Discuss another subject for five minutes. Then ask the founder and listeners to:

1. recall the name unaided;
2. type the exact `.com`;
3. explain what sound made it memorable.

A future finalist should achieve at least 80% exact spelling recall in a small multilingual panel. Wrong-but-plausible spellings are a failure, not a near pass.

## Founder veto

Proceed only when every answer is an unqualified yes:

- Would I naturally and confidently say this every day?
- Would I avoid rehearsing its pronunciation?
- Would I be comfortable telling someone over the phone?
- Would I expect not to spell it repeatedly?

If meaningful doubt remains, continue searching.
