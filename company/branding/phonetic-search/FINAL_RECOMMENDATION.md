# Final Recommendation

## Purpose

This document states the Independent Global Naming Board's clean-slate decision and the actions required before any future adoption.

## Decision

**B. NO NAME MEETS THE STANDARD**

No company name is selected. The founder should **continue searching** and should **not proceed to checkout** for any name in this report.

## Domain status

The five final research domains—`tavalen.com`, `narelon.com`, `ravalen.com`, `darelon.com`, and `renalen.com`—returned no Verisign `.com` RDAP record at **2026-07-12 12:00:21 UTC** and were classified as apparently available. None was purchased, reserved, or taken into a registrar checkout flow.

Domain status can change at any moment and must be reconfirmed during checkout.

## Why no name won

- **Phonetics:** all five can be pronounced, but none has one overwhelmingly obvious cross-market reading.
- **Spelling:** every finalist produced moderate first-hearing alternatives. The brief requires Very Low or Low ambiguity.
- **Memory:** the sounds are recoverable, but exact domain spelling is not consistently recoverable after delay.
- **Conversation:** no finalist felt sufficiently natural and distinctive across founder introductions, phone calls, product endorsements, and long-term company headlines.
- **Conflicts:** deeper review found medical/product, software, company, geographic, or near-brand concerns in four candidates. Narelon remained cleaner but was not strong enough phonetically to win by default.

## Founder veto

The board cannot confidently answer “yes” to all four questions: natural daily use, no pronunciation rehearsal, comfortable phone use, and no repeated spelling. A permanent name should not be adopted because it is merely available.

## Unresolved concerns

- No native-speaker panel was conducted in the eight screened languages.
- No specialist search of phonetic and similar marks by goods/services class was conducted.
- Dynamic trademark, company, app-store, and social databases may contain unindexed records.
- Registrar pricing, premium classification, and checkout availability were not tested.

## Required next steps

1. Begin a second phonetic-first search with blind audio testing before spelling is revealed.
2. Require at least 80% exact spelling recall from a small multilingual listener panel.
3. Let the founder speak each survivor repeatedly in unscripted conversation.
4. Recheck authoritative RDAP and registrar checkout only after a candidate passes those tests.
5. Commission professional trademark clearance in intended markets before incorporation, filing, or substantial brand investment.

Preliminary conflict screening is not trademark clearance. Before substantial brand investment, incorporation under the name, or trademark filing, obtain advice from a qualified trademark attorney in the intended markets.
