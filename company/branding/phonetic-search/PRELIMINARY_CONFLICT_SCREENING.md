# Preliminary Conflict Screening

## Purpose

This document records non-legal, preliminary checks for obvious company, product, trademark, application, and online-identity conflicts.

## Sources and method

Exact-name searches covered general web results and indexed references to company registries, LinkedIn, GitHub, Apple App Store, Google Play, startup directories, software directories, and social platforms. Targeted indexed searches were also run for EUIPO, WIPO, USPTO-related trademark records, and UK trademark results. Bulgarian and other national sources were included where accessible through indexed public results.

No result must be interpreted as clearance. Several official databases are dynamic, incomplete in ordinary web indexes, or require specialist similarity and goods/services analysis.

## Candidate findings

### Tavalen

- Exact searches found unrelated fictional/game usage.
- The name is close in sound and spelling to **Tavalon**, an established tea brand.
- The similarity creates repeat-back and distinctiveness concerns even without a confirmed relevant-class trademark hit.

### Narelon

- No obvious major technology company surfaced in the accessible exact-name search.
- Personal/user-handle use exists.
- The apparently lighter conflict profile does not resolve the name's spelling ambiguity.

### Ravalen

- Ravalen is a Swedish lake/place name.
- Active or historical organization references include Swedish property entities, a Spanish company, education use, and other businesses.
- These are not necessarily technology conflicts, but the name is not clean or uniquely ownable.

### Darelon

- Exact-name business references include a Norwegian sole proprietorship categorized in game/software publishing and a Nigerian company.
- The software-category overlap is a material preliminary conflict signal.

### Renalen

- **Renalen** is used as a branded nutritional/medical product by Meiji-related sources.
- Its medical meaning and product use make it unsuitable regardless of domain status.

## Database result caution

No conclusive exact-name record was found through the limited indexed queries of EUIPO, WIPO, UKIPO, or US trademark sources, but absence from search-engine results is not absence from the registers. Similar marks, phonetic equivalents, local rights, unregistered use, and relevant goods/services classes remain unresolved.

## Conclusion

Renalen and Darelon have direct reasons for rejection. Ravalen has extensive prior use. Tavalen has a meaningful near-name concern. Narelon has the lightest observed conflict profile but does not pass the phonetic-first founder standard.

Preliminary conflict screening is not trademark clearance. Before substantial brand investment, incorporation under a name, or trademark filing, obtain advice from a qualified trademark attorney in the intended markets.
