# Search Criteria

## Purpose

This document defines the phonetic-first standard used to evaluate a permanent, globally credible company name.

## Independence

The search restarted from zero. Orendalis, Noveridian, Talenoria, all earlier finalists, and their naming patterns were excluded. Executive Career OS, Atlas, Career Ledger, Executive Blueprint, and related concepts were treated as products rather than candidate company names.

## Non-negotiable tests

A viable name must be:

- natural to pronounce without rehearsal;
- likely to be spelled correctly after one hearing;
- comfortable in founder, customer, media, workplace, and product sentences;
- broad enough for people and organizations beyond executive careers;
- free of obvious negative meanings in the screened languages;
- supported by an exact matching `.com` that appears normally registrable;
- free of an obvious major or directly relevant conflict in preliminary research.

Preference was given to four-to-eight letters, two syllables, one obvious stress, simple consonant-vowel movement, and no fashionable technology suffix. Three syllables remained eligible only when speech and spelling stayed easy.

## Naming territories

The generation set was balanced across:

1. simple invented words;
2. underused real words;
3. concise meaningful compounds;
4. direction and progress;
5. memory and identity;
6. trust and evidence.

No territory was allowed to supply more than one-third of the initial set. Restricted industry words and fashionable devices were removed before scoring.

## Weighted model

| Criterion | Weight |
| --- | ---: |
| Pronunciation ease | 20% |
| First-hearing spelling ease | 20% |
| Memorability | 15% |
| Conversational quality | 10% |
| Trust and warmth | 10% |
| Global language suitability | 10% |
| Exact `.com` status | 5% |
| Preliminary conflict profile | 5% |
| Long-term expansion | 5% |

Scores are decision aids, not legal or linguistic facts. A serious phonetic defect is disqualifying regardless of total score.

## Research boundaries

This was desk research, not consumer testing, linguistic certification, trademark clearance, or a guarantee of domain availability. No domain was purchased or reserved, no external account was created, and no legal terms or payments were entered.
