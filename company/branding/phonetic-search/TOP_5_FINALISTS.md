## Narelon

- **Pronunciation:** `NAIR-uh-lon`; three syllables; stress on the first.
- **Alternatives heard:** Narelan, Nairalon, Naralon.
- **Rationale:** invented, calm sound with no narrow product meaning.
- **Conversation:** “Atlas is developed by Narelon” is workable; a phone introduction would likely require spelling.
- **Memory:** the opening survives, but the internal vowels do not reliably survive five minutes.
- **Languages:** broadly pronounceable, with first-vowel variation; no obvious negative meaning found.
- **Domain:** `narelon.com` apparently available at 2026-07-12 12:00:21 UTC.
- **Conflict:** no major exact technology conflict surfaced in limited research; personal handle use exists.
- **Strength:** lightest observed conflict profile.
- **Weakness:** does not pass the hear-and-type standard.

## Tavalen

- **Pronunciation:** `TAV-uh-len`; three syllables; stress on the first.
- **Alternatives heard:** Tavalin, Tavallon, Tavalon.
- **Rationale:** invented with a strong opening and balanced rhythm.
- **Conversation:** founder and product sentences sound competent, though generic.
- **Memory:** good rhythm, but likely to be reconstructed as Tavalon.
- **Languages:** pronounceable; vowel treatment varies.
- **Domain:** `tavalen.com` apparently available at 2026-07-12 12:00:21 UTC.
- **Conflict:** near-name concern with established Tavalon; unrelated fictional/game use.
- **Strength:** best conversational rhythm.
- **Weakness:** near-name confusion and constructed tone.

## Darelon

- **Pronunciation:** `DAIR-uh-lon`; three syllables; stress on the first.
- **Alternatives heard:** Daralon, Dairalon, Darelan.

- **Memory:** “dare” helps recall; the remainder does not.
- **Languages:** English pronunciation differs from full-vowel readings elsewhere.
- **Domain:** `darelon.com` apparently available at 2026-07-12 12:00:21 UTC.

## Renalen

- **Pronunciation:** `REN-uh-len`; three syllables; stress on the first.
- **Alternatives heard:** Renalin, Renallon, Renalen.
- **Rationale:** initially survived on rhythm and apparent spelling structure.
- **Conversation:** fluent in isolation but carries a clinical tone after recognition.
- **Memory:** highly memorable for the wrong reason: resemblance to “renal.”
- **Languages:** medical association crosses several European languages.
- **Domain:** `renalen.com` apparently available at 2026-07-12 12:00:21 UTC.
- **Conflict:** established branded nutritional/medical product use.
- **Strength:** easy to pronounce.
- **Weakness:** disqualifying medical association and product conflict.

## Finalist comparison

The numerical tie near the top is not evidence of three winners. It shows that each candidate trades one foundational weakness for another. None passes the founder veto, so the board does not elevate the highest arithmetic score into a recommendation.
