# Brand Analysis

## Strategic territory

The company is not an employment utility. Its durable territory is **human direction amplified by intelligent systems**. The parent brand should therefore suggest orientation, agency, momentum, and long-range possibility without naming a current technology or customer segment.

## Linguistic design rules

- Prefer open vowels and familiar consonant pairs.
- Avoid English-only jokes, silent-letter traps, and sounds that collapse across languages.
- Accept a slightly longer coined mark when it materially improves legal/domain ownability.
- Keep primary stress intuitive: `o-ren-DA-lis`, `no-ve-RI-di-an`.
- Avoid names whose first hearing leads to several plausible spellings.

## Conversation and AI-agent tests

Orendalis performs naturally across the required constructions:

- “I work at Orendalis.”
- “I use Orendalis.”
- “Open Orendalis.”
- “Our company is Orendalis.”
- “The founders of Orendalis.”
- “The CEO of Orendalis.”
- “The Orendalis platform.”
- “Atlas, compare these offers using Orendalis.”
- “Send my Career Passport to Orendalis.”
- “Connect Orendalis.”

The name is sufficiently distinctive for entity recognition and does not collide semantically with a common command. Noveridian and Soleridian are similarly distinctive but consume more speech and screen space. Caldris is the most compact spoken mark but loses on domain control.

## Scoring model

Thirteen criteria were scored 1–10 with equal weights: memorability, pronunciation, premium feeling, trust, scalability, global suitability, conversation, investor friendliness, brandability, domain quality, preliminary trademark safety, AI friendliness, and future expansion. Domain and trademark scores are point-in-time estimates, not legal findings.

## Brand architecture recommendation

Use an endorsed-product architecture initially:

- Executive Career OS — by Orendalis
- Atlas Intelligence — by Orendalis
- Career Passport — by Orendalis

As awareness grows, shift toward a house-of-brands model where the corporate mark is visible in trust, legal, investor, and ecosystem contexts without forcing every product to share its name.
