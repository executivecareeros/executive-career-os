# Orendalis Visual Identity

## Brand idea

Orendalis offers a clearer perspective on executive opportunity. The identity is calm, directional, human, and precise.

## Concept review

Five original directions were evaluated internally:

1. **Open Horizon** — a rising point above a stable horizon; strongest expression of perspective, direction, and opportunity.
2. **Quiet Compass** — a restrained directional needle; clear but too familiar in navigation brands.
3. **Threshold** — an open architectural passage; premium, though less legible at favicon size.
4. **Converging Paths** — two paths meeting at a decision point; meaningful, but visually complex in embroidery.
5. **Human Arc** — a protective arc around a point; warm, but less distinctive from advisory firms.

Open Horizon was selected because it remains recognizable as a website mark, favicon, app icon, social avatar, monochrome stamp, embroidery, and business-card symbol. It contains no AI cliché and does not make a technological claim.

## Core system

- Charcoal: `#22211F` — primary typography and mark field.
- Warm ivory: `#F7F3EC` — primary background.
- Paper: `#FFFDF9` — elevated surfaces.
- Bronze: `#936B3F` — the sole accent for direction and emphasis.
- Border: `#E3DDD3` — quiet structure.

Typography uses a restrained editorial display face for major headings and a highly readable sans serif for interface text. Whitespace is structural. Blue, neon, random status colors, dark panels, and technology motifs are outside the primary system.

## Symbol meaning

The rising point represents perspective and purposeful movement. The open chevron suggests direction without prescribing a single route. The bronze horizon represents a grounded decision threshold. The circle contains the system with calm confidence.

## Usage

Use the full wordmark in navigation, authentication, company documents, and external communication. Use the symbol alone for favicon, app icon, social avatar, monochrome stamp, embroidery, and compact product surfaces. Preserve generous clear space around the symbol and never add effects, gradients, or alternate accent colors.
