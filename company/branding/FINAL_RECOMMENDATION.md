# Final Recommendation

## Recommend advancing **Orendalis** to legal clearance

Orendalis is the best overall company-name candidate for the business currently known as Executive Career OS. It combines human warmth, institutional permanence, global pronunciation, product independence, conversational fluency, and favorable point-in-time exact-domain economics.

It is not a product description. That is the advantage. The word can accumulate meaning over decades while the portfolio expands from executive careers into student, founder, business, identity, and decision-intelligence products.

## Decision rationale

| Requirement | Orendalis assessment |
|---|---|
| Premium global company | Strong; sounds composed rather than promotional |
| Future-proof | Does not name a category, customer, or current technology |
| Speakable and memorable | Four clear syllables; intuitive stress; distinctive rhythm |
| Investor credibility | Sounds credible in financing, partnership, and public-market contexts |
| AI-agent friendliness | Distinct entity token; not confused with a common command |
| Exact `.com` | Registry returned 404 on 12 July 2026; appeared unregistered at that moment |
| Preliminary conflict profile | No obvious exact-name knockout surfaced; Orenda-family review required |
| Portfolio fit | Natural parent for Executive Career OS, Atlas Intelligence, Career Passport, Student OS, Founder OS, and Business OS |

- **Positioning idea:** Intelligent direction for consequential human decisions.

## Go/no-go gates

This recommendation is **conditional**, not authorization to launch.

1. Commission a qualified trademark attorney to clear Orendalis and confusingly similar Orenda-family marks in Nice Classes 9, 35, 41, and 42 across launch jurisdictions.
2. If counsel gives a positive preliminary view, immediately recheck and register `orendalis.com` plus priority country domains and handles.
3. Conduct native-speaker validation with at least two people in each priority language, including adverse meanings and spelling recall.
4. Test the name unprompted with customers and investors: hear → spell, 24-hour recall, trust, and category-expansion exercises.
5. Keep **Noveridian** and **Talenoria** as legal-clearance backups. Do not publicize any candidate before rights and domains are secured.

## If Orendalis fails clearance

Advance Noveridian first, then Talenoria. Do not default to Caldris or Merovia merely because they are shorter: their registered exact `.com` domains create avoidable cost and control risk.
