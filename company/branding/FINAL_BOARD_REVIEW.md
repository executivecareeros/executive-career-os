# Final Independent Board Review: Orendalis

**Review date:** 12 July 2026  
**Decision:** **APPROVE WITH MINOR CONCERNS**

## Executive judgment

Orendalis is strong enough to become the permanent company identity.

It is distinctive, credible in institutional settings, emotionally warm without becoming casual, and broad enough to support products and markets the founders cannot yet predict. It works as a company rather than a feature, app, or industry label. Its minor weaknesses are real but manageable and do not justify reopening the naming process.

**The founders should stop searching and move forward with branding, subject to full legal clearance and securing the domain.**

## Genuine concerns only

### 1. Four syllables

**Concern:** Orendalis is longer in speech than the shortest iconic technology names.  
**Practical impact:** Low. Its rhythm is regular, and many enduring company names exceed two syllables. The name does not feel cumbersome in the tested sentences.  
**Recommendation:** Accept. Do not force a nickname into public use.

### 2. First-hearing spelling is good, not perfect

**Concern:** A minority of listeners may omit the `d` or vary the middle vowel before seeing the word.  
**Practical impact:** Low to moderate during early awareness; low after exposure.  
**Recommendation:** Accept. Pair spoken introductions with the written wordmark during launch, standardize pronunciation internally, and test voice-assistant aliases.

### 3. The name has no universally understood dictionary meaning

**Concern:** Orendalis does not explain itself.  
**Practical impact:** Low and strategically positive for a parent company. It requires brand investment but avoids category confinement.  
**Recommendation:** Accept. Build meaning through product quality, company behavior, and a consistent narrative; do not publish a contrived etymology as fact.

### 4. Trademark clearance is not complete

**Concern:** Similarity to Orenda-family names may create conflicts depending on jurisdiction, class, goods/services, and commercial impression.  
**Practical impact:** Potentially high if counsel finds a material conflict; currently unknown, not presumed.  
**Recommendation:** Treat this as the only adoption gate. Commission a comprehensive attorney-led search before public launch or major identity expenditure.
