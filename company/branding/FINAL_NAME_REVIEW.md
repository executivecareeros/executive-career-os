# Final Name Review

## Board verdict

**Keep Orendalis.**

The second search did not produce a name that clearly surpasses it. Orenalis came closest and is shorter, but shortening alone is not improvement. The missing consonant costs the word authority, distinctiveness, and memory structure.

## Sentence stress test

- “I use Orendalis.” — natural.
- “The founders of Orendalis.” — credible.
- “NASDAQ: ORDL” — believable hypothetical ticker; no ticker availability claim is made.
- “I’ve been using Orendalis for years.” — memorable.
- “Atlas, open Orendalis.” — unambiguous.
- “The CEO of Orendalis.” — global-company cadence.
- “Orendalis acquires…” — authoritative.
- “Orendalis announces…” — natural.
- “Welcome to Orendalis.” — warm.
- “Powered by Orendalis.” — credible.
- “The Orendalis ecosystem.” — scalable.
- **“Welcome to the twentieth anniversary of Orendalis.”** — ceremonial without sounding inflated.

## Why Orendalis beats the three incumbents

- **Versus Noveridian:** Orendalis is warmer, easier to say, and less engineered. Noveridian sounds like a strategic framework; Orendalis sounds like an institution.
- **Versus Talenoria:** Orendalis does not imply talent or employment. It can enter any industry without semantic repair.
- **Versus its previous self:** The second round tested whether the recommendation was merely familiarity bias. A near-minimal challenger, Orenalis, exposed why the original construction works: the `d` is not clutter; it is the name’s backbone.

## Domain and conflict posture

`orendalis.com` returned no Verisign `.com` registry record when checked on 12 July 2026. This is a point-in-time availability signal, not a reservation. No obvious exact-name major software company or global product surfaced in preliminary web screening. Similar Orenda-family marks remain the main legal issue for counsel.

## Decision rule

Do not reopen naming because another acceptable word appears. Reopen only if qualified counsel finds an unacceptable legal conflict, native-language research finds a serious adverse meaning, or the exact domain cannot be secured before adoption.
