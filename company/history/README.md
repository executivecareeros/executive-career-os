# Company History and Founder Archive

> Purpose: Preserve how Executive Career OS was conceived, built, governed, and changed from Day One.

Company history matters because future founders, employees, investors, board members, and historians need more than a list of code changes. They need the intent, alternatives, turning points, challenges, and lessons behind the company.

Git history records technical artifacts and commit sequence. Company history records human purpose, strategic reasoning, institutional context, customer and business milestones, and what was learned. Neither replaces the other.

## Archival practice

- Preserve original dates, sources, commit references, and uncertainty.
- Use placeholders where facts are unknown; never create a cleaner story than the evidence supports.
- Update after a material release, company decision, customer/business first, financing, leadership change, incident, or annual review.
- The founder owns the archive until a Corporate Secretary or Company Historian is assigned.
- Material entries should be reviewed by the decision owner and linked to source evidence.
- Corrections should be dated and explained; historical entries should not disappear silently.

Review the timeline and decision history quarterly, release history after each release, and the full archive annually.
