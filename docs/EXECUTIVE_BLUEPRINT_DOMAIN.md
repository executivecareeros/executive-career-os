# Executive Blueprint Domain
> **Purpose:** Define the versioned personalization context that future Atlas decisions must use and explain.
## Model
The Blueprint records identity, positioning, horizons, objectives, roles, industries, geographies, work arrangements, mobility, company preferences, compensation, leadership, board goals, risk, negotiation, lifestyle constraints, values, and non-negotiable principles. Structured preferences carry priority, weight, requirement, direction, confidence, evidence, validity, confirmation, revision, notes, and demo status.
## Revision, Evidence, and Confidence
Approved values are never silently overwritten. Each change creates a revision with previous and new values, reason, actor, evidence, confidence, effective date, approval state, and an optional Career Ledger reference. Evidence remains independently attributable; confidence describes support, not truth.
## Conflicts and Completeness
Deterministic rules surface contradictions without resolving them. Explanations identify affected fields and principles. Completeness combines required foundations, confirmed sections, recency, evidence, and unresolved conflicts into Not Started, Basic, Developing, Strong, or Decision Ready. It is not an AI score.
## Onboarding and Executive DNA
Guided questions capture strategic intent without AI. Executive DNA expresses career preferences across paired dimensions; it is not clinical or diagnostic. Decision-priority weights allow future recommendations to reflect explicit trade-offs.
## Integration Boundaries
Opportunity and company matching expose matches, violations, non-negotiable conflicts, missing information, and the revision used. Discovery receives a revision-scoped search context. Agent context carries priorities, constraints, exclusions, conflicts, confidence, evidence, and confirmation date. Atlas must eventually cite the Blueprint facts that influenced each recommendation.
## Demonstration Boundary
Sprint 11 uses fictional local data and temporary client state. There is no persistence, authentication, AI, external integration, or automatic conflict resolution.
