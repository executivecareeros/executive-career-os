# Career Replay

> **Purpose:** Describe how Executive Career OS turns permanent historical memory into useful reflection and decision support.

## Career Replay

Career Replay presents an executive career as a coherent, navigable narrative rather than a collection of disconnected records.

It should help an executive revisit what happened, understand why decisions were made, compare expectations with outcomes, and apply lessons to the next decision.

## Career Ledger

The Career Ledger is the append-only historical record beneath Career Replay. It preserves significant events, actors, evidence, rationale, values, documents, decisions, and outcomes.

Corrections add context; they do not erase the earlier record.

## Historical Memory

Historical Memory connects events across opportunities, companies, applications, relationships, interviews, documents, compensation, and strategy.

Its purpose is continuity: important context should remain available after a process closes or a current value changes.

## Decision Memory

Decision Memory preserves the alternatives considered, evidence available, recommendation made, confidence held, rationale recorded, and outcome observed.

This allows future decisions to benefit from earlier judgment without pretending that circumstances are identical.

## Interview Memory

Interview Memory retains preparation, participants, questions, signals, commitments, impressions, feedback, and lessons across interview processes.

## Compensation History

Compensation History preserves advertised values, expectations, requests, budgets, offers, counteroffers, final packages, decisions, and the reasons behind them.

It should support better preparation without reducing compensation to a single number.

## Lessons Learned

Career Replay should make it possible to record and retrieve lessons: what proved true, what was missed, which assumptions changed, and which patterns deserve attention.

Lessons should remain linked to their evidence and context.

## Permanent Archive

The archive is durable memory, not a place where information goes to disappear. Archived material remains retrievable, related, and available for authorized reflection.

Future retention and deletion policies must balance historical value, privacy, legal obligations, and executive control.
