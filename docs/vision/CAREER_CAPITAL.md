# Career Capital

> **Purpose:** Define a strategic framework for understanding the assets and momentum accumulated across an executive career.

## Definition

Career Capital is the combined stock of credibility, capability, relationships, options, and demonstrated value that expands an executive’s ability to choose and succeed in future work.

It is multidimensional. Compensation or title alone cannot describe it.

## Proposed Dimensions

### Leadership

Evidence of leading people, organizations, transformation, and consequential decisions.

### Compensation

The economic value the market has assigned to the executive’s contribution, including structure and quality—not only headline amount.

### Executive Brand

The clarity, credibility, and distinctiveness of how the executive is understood by relevant markets and communities.

### Network

The strength, relevance, reciprocity, and reach of trusted professional relationships.

### Market Demand

The degree to which the executive’s capabilities address important, current, and emerging needs.

### Geographic Reach

The ability to operate credibly across markets, cultures, regulatory contexts, and regions.

### Board Experience

Governance judgment, fiduciary exposure, committee experience, and credibility with boards and investors.

### Industry Diversity

The breadth and transferability of experience across sectors, business models, ownership structures, and company stages.

### Career Momentum

The direction, pace, coherence, and cumulative strength of recent career developments.

### Learning

The executive’s demonstrated ability to acquire, apply, and compound new knowledge and capability.

## No Formula Yet

These dimensions are a proposed language for reflection and decision support. They are not yet a scoring formula. Future measurement should be evidence-based, explainable, context-sensitive, and resistant to false precision.
