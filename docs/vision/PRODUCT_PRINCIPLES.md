# Product Principles

> **Purpose:** Establish the standards used to evaluate product decisions across Executive Career OS.

## Keep the Core Useful and Owned

Free access must remain genuinely useful. Career Ledger, archive, and exported history must never be held hostage by a premium tier. Paid plans may deepen intelligence, memory, guidance, and supervised autonomy, but limits and availability must remain transparent.

## Improve Executive Decision-Making

Every feature must improve an executive’s ability to understand context, evaluate alternatives, or act with greater clarity. Activity without decision value is not progress.

## Make Recommendations Explainable

Every recommendation must show its reasoning, evidence, assumptions, confidence, and important counterarguments. Conclusions without explanation do not deserve trust.

## Never Lose History

Important career context must survive status changes, document revisions, negotiations, closed processes, and changing recommendations.

## Preserve Important Career Events

Decisions, evidence, conversations, milestones, outcomes, and lessons should form a coherent historical record. Archive means retained context, not disappearance.

## Build Trust Before Automation

Automation should follow demonstrated reliability, clear user control, and reversible behavior. The platform must earn permission to act.

## Augment Judgment

AI should expand perspective, recall relevant history, structure evidence, and challenge thinking. It should not replace executive accountability or present uncertainty as fact.

## Respect Context and Privacy

Career and compensation information is sensitive. Access, retention, visibility, and future integrations must reflect that sensitivity.

## Prefer Clarity

The product should reduce cognitive load, use language precisely, and distinguish verified information from estimates, inference, and demonstration data.

## Design for the Career, Not the Transaction

Individual opportunities matter, but the platform should optimize for the long-term career: reputation, relationships, learning, compensation, optionality, impact, and personal constraints.

## Keep These Principles Alive

These principles should be revisited when product decisions expose new tensions. Changes should clarify the standard, not weaken it for convenience.
