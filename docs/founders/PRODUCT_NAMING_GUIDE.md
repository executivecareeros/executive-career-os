# Product Naming Guide

> **Purpose:** Maintain a single, durable vocabulary for the product, its domains, and its decisions.

## Naming Principles

Use the canonical name exactly. Capitalization signals a defined product concept. Prefer specific domain language over generic software terms.

## Canonical Vocabulary

| Canonical term | Meaning | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Executive Career OS** | The company’s integrated product | career app, job app, platform OS |
| **Executive** | The person whose career is served | candidate, applicant, user when career context matters |
| **Atlas** | The trusted intelligence and decision-support partner | bot, chatbot, copilot, algorithm |
| **Executive Blueprint** | The versioned statement of identity, direction, preferences, constraints, and principles | profile, persona, settings profile |
| **Executive Brief** | A prioritized, evidence-based review of what changed and what needs attention | feed, news summary, digest when referring to the product experience |
| **Career Ledger** | The permanent, attributable career record | activity log, database history |
| **Career Replay** | A reconstruction and review of career history and decisions | timeline replay, audit playback |
| **Career Capital** | The accumulated assets and momentum that expand future career choice | career score, employability score |
| **Opportunity Intelligence** | Evidence and assessment concerning a career opportunity | job intelligence, vacancy analytics |
| **Company Intelligence** | Evidence and assessment concerning an organization | employer profile, company card |
| **Applications CRM** | The executive’s relationship and process workspace for active applications | ATS, application tracker |
| **Discovery Engine** | The source-agnostic acquisition and normalization layer | job scraper, LinkedIn search |
| **Knowledge Network** | The attributable network of executive-market signals and relationships | news feed, knowledge graph when the broader product concept is meant |
| **Compensation Intelligence** | The dated record and analysis of compensation expectations, ranges, and offers | salary calculator |
| **Assessment** | A structured evaluation of evidence | verdict, opinion |
| **Recommendation** | A proposed action supported by an assessment | answer, command, decision when Atlas is not the decision-maker |
| **Confidence** | The strength and completeness of supporting context | success probability, certainty |
| **Archive** | Retained records removed from active work | trash, deleted items |

## Usage Rules

- Use **opportunity** for a potential career move; use **job** only when referring to a source’s job record or the generic labor-market concept.
- Use **executive** instead of **candidate** unless describing another party’s hiring process.
- Use **decision** for the executive’s choice. Atlas produces a **recommendation**.
- Use **recorded**, **corrected**, **archived**, and **restored** for historical actions. Never imply deletion when history is retained.
- Do not shorten Executive Blueprint to “profile” or Career Ledger to “history” in headings.
- Plan names are **Free**, **Atlas Pro**, and **Atlas Executive**.

## Creating New Names

A new product name should describe an enduring executive need, remain understandable without explanation, and fit the existing system. Do not create a branded term when a canonical term already covers the concept.
