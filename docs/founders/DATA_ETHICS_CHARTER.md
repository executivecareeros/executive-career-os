# Data Ethics Charter

> **Purpose:** Define the obligations that govern collection, memory, analysis, use, and stewardship of executive career data.

## Ownership

The executive owns their career data. Product access, commercial status, or provider relationships must not weaken that ownership.

## Purpose Limitation

Data should be collected and used for clear, legitimate purposes that serve the executive. Information gathered for one purpose must not be repurposed silently.

## Informed Consent

Consent must be understandable, specific, and revocable. Sensitive collection, external sharing, recordings, and consequential automated actions require meaningful choice.

## Historical Integrity

Important history must not be silently changed. Corrections, interpretations, and new evidence should be attributable and appended so prior context remains recoverable.

## Sensitive Information

Compensation, family constraints, health-related context, work authorization, interview notes, documents, communications, and personal principles require heightened protection and restricted access.

## Data Minimization

Use the least information necessary. Do not collect data because it may become useful later. Do not expose entire records when a narrow fact is sufficient.

## Explainability

Material recommendations must identify the data and rules that influenced them. Executives must be able to challenge evidence, assumptions, and conclusions.

## Portability

Executives must be able to obtain their career record in a usable form. Their history must not be held hostage by a plan, provider, or proprietary interface.

## Privacy and Security

Privacy must be designed into access, retention, deletion, provider use, and product defaults. Security controls must be proportionate to the sensitivity and consequence of the data.

## Responsible Intelligence

AI may assist with explanation, synthesis, and preparation. It must not fabricate facts, conceal uncertainty, determine personal principles, or replace human judgment in consequential decisions.

## Human Oversight

The executive retains final authority. Material external actions require clear scope, review, and the ability to stop or reverse them where possible.

## Fairness and Dignity

Data must not be used to manipulate, diminish, stereotype, or exploit an executive. Inferences about personality, protected characteristics, or personal circumstances require exceptional restraint and legitimate purpose.

## Retention and Deletion

Retention must be deliberate and visible. The value of career memory does not justify indefinite storage without choice. Deletion and legal retention obligations must be clearly distinguished from archival history.

## Accountability

Access, changes, provider disclosures, and agent actions must be traceable. Violations of this charter are product failures, not merely compliance issues.
