# Product Entitlements
> **Purpose:** Define transparent product-access boundaries without introducing billing or weakening ownership of career history.
## Plans
**Free** includes the useful core: dashboard, workspaces, Career Ledger and Replay, compensation tracking, Executive Blueprint, manual entry, basic reports, and limited Atlas previews.
**Atlas Pro** may add deeper intelligence, memory, document guidance, interview and compensation guidance, Daily Briefs, multi-source discovery, and Blueprint-aware recommendations.
**Atlas Executive** may add supervised autonomous monitoring, multi-step workflows, simulations, voice, negotiation rehearsal, priority processing, integrations, and concierge support.
Planned capabilities are definitions, not claims of availability.
## Feature Registry and Access
One registry defines key, title, description, category, minimum plan, lifecycle status, allowance, period, upgrade explanation, and dependencies. Pure helpers return access, remaining usage, minimum plan, and a human-readable reason. UI code does not contain scattered plan checks.
## Data-Access Principle
The archive, Career Ledger, and exported history remain accessible to their owner regardless of plan. Limits must be explicit. Atlas must never imply that planned or unavailable functionality is active.
## Future Billing Boundary
Entitlements are independent of billing. A future billing provider may supply plan state, but it must not own feature definitions or career data. Sprint 11 includes no payments, checkout, subscriptions, or live controls.
## Demonstration Limitations
The active plan and counters are local demonstration records. No usage is persisted or enforced across sessions.
